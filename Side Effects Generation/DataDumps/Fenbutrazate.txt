'''Fenbutrazate''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known as '''phenbutrazate''' ([[British Approved Name|BAN]]), is a [[psychostimulant]] used as an [[appetite suppressant]] under the trade names '''Cafilon''', '''Filon''', and '''Sabacid''' in [[Europe]], [[Japan]], and [[Hong Kong]].<ref name="isbn0-412-46630-9">{{cite book | author = Triggle, D. J. | title = Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents | publisher = Chapman & Hall / CRC | location = Boca Raton | year = 1996 | page = 875 | isbn = 0-412-46630-9 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=DeX7jgInYFMC&lpg=PA875&as_brr=3&pg=PA875 }}</ref><ref name="pmid2874968">{{cite journal | author = Keup, W. | title = Use, indications and distribution in different countries of the stimulant and hallucinogenic amphetamine derivatives under consideration by WHO | journal = Drug and Alcohol Dependence | year = 1986 | volume = 17 | issue = 2–3 | pages = 169–192 | pmid = 2874968 | doi = 10.1016/0376-8716(86)90007-4 }}</ref> It is a [[chemical derivative|derivative]] of [[phenmetrazine]] and may function as a [[prodrug]] to it similarly to [[phendimetrazine]].<ref name="urlwhqlibdoc.who.int">{{cite web | url = http://whqlibdoc.who.int/trs/WHO_TRS_729.pdf | title = WHO Expert Committee on Drug Dependence - 22nd Report | format = pdf | year = 1985 | series = World Health Organization Technical Report Series | volume = 729 | publisher = World Health Organization | location = Geneva | isbn = 9241207299 | issn = 0512-3054 }}</ref> It is not known to be a [[controlled substance]] anywhere in the world yet.
