'''Dextranomer''' (trade name '''Debrisan''', '''Exudex<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfRL/rl.cfm?lid=511319&lpcd=KOZ|title=Establishment Registration & Device Listing|publisher=}}</ref>''') is a [[cicatrizant]] used in dressings for wound healing,<ref>{{cite journal | vauthors = Parulkar BG, Sobti MK, Pardanani DS | title = Dextranomer dressing in the treatment of infected wounds and cutaneous ulcers | journal = J Postgrad Med | year = 1985 | volume = 31 | issue = 1 | pages = 28–33| url = http://www.jpgmonline.com/text.asp?1985/31/1/28/5431}}</ref> and in pharmaceutical products to treat [[fecal incontinence]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/news/solesta-gel-approved-fecal-incontinence-31669.html|title=Solesta Gel Approved for Fecal Incontinence - Drugs.com MedNews|publisher=}}</ref>  It consists of [[dextran]] polymer chains cross-linked into a three-dimensional network.<ref>[http://solestainfo.com/docs/solesta_label.pdf Solesta Package Insert]</ref>

==Solesta==
Solesta is the marketed brand name of a system of injections of dextranomer in stabilized [[sodium hyaluronate]] for use in fecal incontinence (FI).<ref name="Hoy 2012">{{cite journal|last=Hoy|first=SM|title=Dextranomer in stabilized sodium hyaluronate (Solesta): in adults with faecal incontinence.|journal=Drugs|date=Aug 20, 2012|volume=72|issue=12|pages=1671–8|pmid=22845415|doi=10.2165/11209030-000000000-00000}}</ref>  This preparation is a biocompatible gel, for use as a [[Fecal incontinence#Perianal injectable bulking agents|perianal injectable bulking agent]]. The system is intended to be injected in the submucosal layer of the proximal [[anal canal]] (i.e. above the level of the [[dentate line]]).
