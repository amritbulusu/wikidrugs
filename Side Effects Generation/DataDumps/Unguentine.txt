'''Unguentine''' is an over the counter [[topical]] [[antiseptic]] [[ointment]] produced by Lee Pharmaceuticals. It was originally introduced in 1893 as the first antiseptic surgical dressing ointment, by [[Norwich Pharmaceuticals]] who later brought Pepto Bismol to market.

It is used for treating the pain of minor burns, poison ivy, poison oak, itching, minor skin wounds or insect bites.<ref>http://www.webmd.com/drugs/drug-61840-Unguentine+Top.aspx?drugid=61840&pagenumber=4</ref> The active ingredients are [[camphor]] (3%), [[phenol]] (2.5%), [[tannic acid]] (2.2%) and [[zinc oxide]] (6.6%).<ref>http://www.drugstore.com/unguentine-unquentine-antiseptic-skin-protectant-ointment/qxp391961</ref> 

TV ads on ''[[The Hollywood Palace]]'' in 1967 mention the active ingredient as [[diperodon]].<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K7jnirwhUjg Hollywood Palace - Gene Barry hosts, with Mort Sahl & Jack E Leonard]</ref> Earlier advertisements list "dianestrol" as its "secret ingredient".
