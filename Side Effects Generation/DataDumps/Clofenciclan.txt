'''Clofenciclan''' ('''Chlorphencyclan'''; '''Tonquil''', '''Vesitan''') is a [[dopamine]]-releasing agent developed by Boehringer & Soehne in the 1950s.<ref>{{US patent|3254083}}</ref> It proved unpopular as a treatment because of its pronounced [[stimulant]] activity.<ref>Poeldinger W. Therapeutic experiences with thiopropazate and with a combination of thiopropazate and chlorphencyclan (Vesitan) in psychiatry. (German). ''Praxis''. 1962 Jan 18;51:73-8. {{PMID|14487367}}</ref>
