'''Alphamethadol''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), or '''α-methadol''', also known as '''alfametadol''', is a [[Chemical synthesis|synthetic]] [[opioid]] [[analgesic]].<ref name="Commission2002">{{cite book | author = British Pharmacopoeia Commission | title = British Approved Names 2002 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=zruxjioUFngC&pg=PA9 | accessdate = 15 May 2012 | date = 1 February 2002 | publisher = The Stationery Office | isbn = 978-0-11-322558-3 | page = 9}}</ref> It is an [[isomer]] of [[dimepheptanol]] (methadol), the other being [[betamethadol]] (β-methadol).<ref name="Crime2006">{{cite book | author = United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime | title = Dictionnaire Multilingue Des Stupéfiants Et Des Substances Psychotropes Placés Sous Contrôle International | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=RPAphv7h4-IC&pg=PA103 | accessdate = 15 May 2012 | year = 2006 | publisher = United Nations Publications | isbn = 978-92-1-048117-5 | page = 103}}</ref> Alphamethadol is composed of two isomers itself, <small>L</small>-α-methadol, and <small>D</small>-α-methadol.<ref name="Crime2006" /> The former compound, <small>L</small>-α-methadol, is an important [[active metabolite]] of [[levacetylmethadol]] (LAAM),{{Citation needed|date=May 2012}} an opioid substitute drug that is used clinically. Both of alphamethadol's isomers bind to and activate the [[μ-opioid receptor]] and are active as opioid analgesics,<ref name="pmid60774">{{cite journal | vauthors = Horng JS, Smits SE, Wong DT | title = The binding of the optical isomers of methadone, alpha-methadol, alpha-acetylmethadol and their N-demethylated derivatives to the opiate receptors of rat brain | journal = Research Communications in Chemical Pathology and Pharmacology | volume = 14 | issue = 4 | pages = 621–9 |date=August 1976 | pmid = 60774 | doi = | url = }}</ref> similarly to those of [[alphacetylmethadol]] (α-acetylmethadol).<ref name="pmid12373424">{{cite journal | vauthors = Newman JL, Vann RE, May EL, Beardsley PM | title = Heroin discriminative stimulus effects of methadone, LAAM and other isomers of acetylmethadol in rats | journal = Psychopharmacology | volume = 164 | issue = 1 | pages = 108–14 |date=October 2002 | pmid = 12373424 | doi = 10.1007/s00213-002-1198-8 | url = https://dx.doi.org/10.1007/s00213-002-1198-8}}</ref>

==Legal Status==

===Australia===
Alphamethadol is considered a Schedule 9 prohibited substance in Australia under the [[Standard for the Uniform Scheduling of Medicines and Poisons|Poisons Standard]] (February 2017).<ref name="Poisons Standard">Poisons Standard October 2015 https://www.legislation.gov.au/Details/F2017L00057</ref> A Schedule 9 substance is a substance which may be abused or misused, the manufacture, possession, sale or use of which should be prohibited by law except when required for medical or scientific research, or for analytical, teaching or training purposes with approval of Commonwealth and/or State or Territory Health Authorities.<ref name="Poisons Standard" />
