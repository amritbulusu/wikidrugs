'''Cyclopregnol''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known as '''neurosterone''', as well as '''6β-hydroxy-3:5-cyclopregnan-20-one''', is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]] [[pregnane]] [[steroid]] which was developed in the 1950s as a "[[psychotropic agent]]" for the treatment of [[mental disorder]]s but was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA646|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=646–}}</ref><ref name="PatelPetrow1957">{{cite journal|last1=Patel|first1=D. K.|last2=Petrow|first2=V.|last3=Stuart-Webb|first3=I. A.|title=133. 6β-Hydroxy-3 : 5-cyclopregnan-20-one and some related compounds|journal=J. Chem. Soc.|volume=0|issue=0|year=1957|pages=665–668|issn=0368-1769|doi=10.1039/JR9570000665}}</ref><ref name="pmid13481595">{{cite journal | vauthors = HARDWICK SW, PEARSE JJ, PETROW V | title = 6 beta-Hydroxy-3:5-cyclopregnan-20-one in mental states | journal = J Ment Sci | volume = 103 | issue = 433 | pages = 835–9 | year = 1957 | pmid = 13481595 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid4874165">{{cite journal | vauthors = Loranger AW | title = Treatment of acute mental disorders with an adrenal steroid | journal = Br J Psychiatry | volume = 114 | issue = 512 | pages = 843–4 | year = 1968 | pmid = 4874165 | doi = | url = }}</ref> Although an initial small clinical study found effectiveness,<ref name="pmid13481595" /> a subsequent, larger and more rigorous study found that cyclopregnol was no more effective than [[placebo]] and was clearly inferior to [[chlorpromazine]].<ref name="pmid4874165" /><ref name="AcademicPress1969">{{cite book|title=Annual Reports in Medicinal Chemistry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rDUMWpvOgjkC&pg=PA3|year=1969|publisher=Academic Press|isbn=978-0-08-058348-8|pages=3–}}</ref>
