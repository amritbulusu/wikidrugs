'''5F-EMB-PINACA''' (also known as '''5F-AEB''') is an [[indazole]]-based synthetic [[cannabinoid]] from the indazole-3-carboxamide family that has been sold online as a [[designer drug]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.caymanchem.com/app/template/Product.vm/catalog/17735 | title=5F-AEB | publisher=Cayman Chemical | accessdate=10 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.unodc.org/LSS/Announcement/Details/d05ee4c0-c835-4055-b042-df0a15f16f49 | title=UNODC EWA: Fifteen novel synthetic cannabinoids reported to UNODC EWA since the beginning of the year | publisher=United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) | accessdate=10 August 2015}}</ref>

It was first reported by the [[European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction|EMCDDA]] as part of a seizure of 149 grams of white powder in Sweden in April 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://forum.goeg.at/EwsForum/default.aspx?g=posts&m=237 | title=EWS_EU / AT: checkit; neue psychoakt. Subst. | publisher=Gesundheit Österreich GmbH | accessdate=10 August 2015}}</ref>

== Legal status ==
5F-EMB-PINACA is illegal in Sweden as of 26. January 2016.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.folkhalsomyndigheten.se/nyheter-och-press/nyhetsarkiv/2015/november/31-nya-amnen-kan-klassas-som-narkotika-eller-halsofarlig-vara/ | title=31 nya ämnen kan klassas som narkotika eller hälsofarlig vara | publisher=Folkhälsomyndigheten | language=Swedish | date=November 2015}}</ref>
