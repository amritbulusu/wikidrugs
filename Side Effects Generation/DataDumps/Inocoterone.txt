'''Inocoterone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) is a [[steroid]]-like [[nonsteroidal antiandrogen]] (NSAA) that was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA686|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=686–}}</ref><ref name="Conn2010">{{cite book|author=P. Michael Conn|title=Techniques in Confocal Microscopy|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=cwFowzAniuUC&pg=PA215|date=23 July 2010|publisher=Academic Press|isbn=978-0-12-384659-4|pages=215–}}</ref> An [[acetate]] [[ester]], [[inocoterone acetate]], shows greater antiandrogen activity and was developed as a [[topical medication]] for the treatment of [[acne]] but showed only modest [[effectiveness]] in [[clinical trial]]s and similarly was never marketed.<ref name="AcademicPress1987">{{cite book|title=Annual Reports in Medicinal Chemistry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oJeTSJveeuAC&pg=PA358|date=2 September 1987|publisher=Academic Press|isbn=978-0-08-058366-2|pages=358–}}</ref>
