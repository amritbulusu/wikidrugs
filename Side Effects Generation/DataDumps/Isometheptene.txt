'''Isometheptene''' (usually as '''isometheptene [[mucic acid|mucate]]''') is a [[sympathomimetic amine]] sometimes used in the treatment of [[migraine]]s and tension headaches due to its [[vasoconstrictor|vasoconstricting]] properties; that is, it causes [[constriction]] (narrowing) of [[blood vessel]]s ([[arteries]] and [[veins]]). Along with [[paracetamol]] and [[dichloralphenazone]], it is one of the constituents of [[Amidrine]].

== Chemistry ==
Isometheptene is a [[Saturation (chemistry)|monounsaturated]] [[aliphatic]] [[secondary amine]].

== Mechanism of action ==
Isometheptene's vasoconstricting properties arise through activation of the [[sympathetic nervous system]] via [[epinephrine]] and [[norepinephrine]]. These compounds elicit [[Smooth muscle tissue|smooth muscle]] activation leading to vasoconstriction by interacting with cell surface [[Adrenergic receptor|adrenergic receptors.]]<ref>{{Cite journal|title = Isometheptene|url = http://www.drugbank.ca/drugs/DB06706}}</ref>
