'''Aleph''', '''DOT''', or '''2,5-di[[methoxy]]-4-[[methyl]][[Thio-|thio]][[amphetamine]]''', is a [[psychedelic drug|psychedelic]] [[Psychedelics, dissociatives and deliriants|hallucinogenic drug]] and a [[substituted amphetamine]] of the [[phenethylamine]] class of compounds, which can be used as an [[entheogen]].  It was first synthesized by [[Alexander Shulgin]]. In his book ''[[PiHKAL|PiHKAL (Phenethylamines i Have Known And Loved)]]'', Shulgin lists the dosage range as 5–10&nbsp;mg.<ref>{{CitePiHKAL}}</ref>  According to Shulgin, the effects of aleph typically last for 6 to 8 hours.

It has weak MAO-A inhibition activity with an [[IC50|IC<sub>50</sub>]] of 5.2&nbsp;mmol. For reference, [[amphetamine]] has an IC<sub>50</sub> of 11 and [[4-methylthioamphetamine]] has a value of 0.2.<ref>{{cite journal|doi=10.1021/jm0493109 |pmid=15801832 |title=Sulfur-Substituted α-Alkyl Phenethylamines as Selective and Reversible MAO-A Inhibitors:  Biological Activities, CoMFA Analysis, and Active Site Modeling |journal=Journal of Medicinal Chemistry |volume=48 |issue=7 |pages=2407 |year=2005 |last1=Gallardo-Godoy |first1=Alejandra |last2=Fierro |first2=Angélica |last3=McLean |first3=Thomas H. |last4=Castillo |first4=Mariano |last5=Cassels |first5=Bruce K. |last6=Reyes-Parada |first6=Miguel |last7=Nichols |first7=David E. }}</ref> A lower number indicates a stronger inhibitor.
