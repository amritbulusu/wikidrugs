'''Clemizole''' is an [[H1 antagonist|H<sub>1</sub> antagonist]].

==Synthesis==
Benzimidazoles substituted with an alkylamine at position 2 have a venerable history as H<sub>1</sub> antihistaminic agents. The standard starting material for many benzimidazoles consists of [[phenylenediamine]], or its derivatives.
[[File:Clemizole synthesis.svg|500px|center|thumb|Clemizole synthesis:<ref>{{Cite journal | doi = 10.1002/jlac.19525750204| title = Zur Darstellung der Benzimidazole| journal = Justus Liebigs Annalen der Chemie| volume = 575| issue = 2| pages = 162| year = 1952| last1 = Jerchel | first1 = D. | last2 = Fischer | first2 = H. | last3 = Kracht | first3 = M. }}</ref><ref>{{Cite patent|GB|703272}}; M. Schenck, W. Heinz, {{US patent|2689853}} (both 1954 to [[Schering AG]]).</ref>]]
Reaction of that compound with [[chloroacetic acid]] can be rationalized by invoking initial formation of the chloromethyl amide. [[Imide]] formation with the remaining free amino group closes the ring to afford 2-chloromethyl benzimidazole ('''3'''). Displacement of halogen with [[pyrrolidine]] affords the alkylation product. The proton on the fused imidazole nitrogen is then removed by reaction with [[sodium hydride]]. Treatment of the resulting anion with Î±,4-dichlorotoluene gives the H<sub>1</sub> antihistaminic agent clemizole ('''5''').
