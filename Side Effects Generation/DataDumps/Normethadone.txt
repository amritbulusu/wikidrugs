'''Normethadone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]]; brand names '''Cophylac''', '''Dacartil''', '''Eucopon''', '''Mepidon''', '''Noramidone''', '''Normedon''', and others), also known as '''desmethylmethadone''' or '''phenyldimazone''', is a [[Chemical synthesis|synthetic]] [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] and [[antitussive]] agent.{{citation needed|date=September 2015}}
Normethadone is listed under the Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs 1961 and is a Schedule I Narcotic controlled substance in the United States, with a DEA ACSCN of 9635 and an annual manufacturing quota of 2 grams.<ref>http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/fed_regs/quotas/2014/fr0825.htm</ref>  The salts in use are the hydrobromide (free base conversion ratio 0.785), hydrochloride (0.890), methyliodide (0.675), oxalate (0.766), picrate (0.563), and the 2,6-ditertbutylnapthalindisulphonate (0.480) <ref>A-Z Encyclopaedia of Alcohol & Drug Abuse, pp 470</ref>
