'''Iscotrizinol''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]],<ref>[https://searchusan.ama-assn.org/undefined/documentDownload?uri=%2Funstructured%2Fbinary%2Fusan%2Fiscotrizinol.pdf Statement on a nonproprietary name adopted by the USAN Council]</ref> [[International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients|INCI]] diethylhexyl butamido triazone) is an [[organic compound]] used in [[sunscreen]]s to absorb UVB and some [[Ultraviolet|UVA]] radiation <ref>[http://incidecoder.com/ingredients/diethylhexyl-butamido-triazone incidecoder.com: Diethylhexyl Butamido Triazone]</ref> with a peak protection at 310nm.<ref name="CouteauJune2007">{{cite journal
| title = Study of the efficacy of 18 sun filters authorized in European Union tested in vitro.
| date = June 2007
| vauthors = Couteau C, Pommier M, Paparis E, Coiffard LJ
| journal = Pharmazie| volume=62 | pages=449-452
| pmid = 17663193
| doi = 10.1691/ph.2007.6.6247}}</ref> It is one of the most photostable chemical sunscreens known today with 25 hours required to loose 10% of its SPF protection ability.<ref name="CouteauMay2007">{{cite journal
| title = Study of the photostability of 18 sunscreens in creams by measuring the SPF in vitro
| date = May 2007
| vauthors = Couteau C, Faure A, Fortin J, Paparis E, Coiffard LJ
| journal = Journal of Pharmaceutical and Biomedical Analysis| volume=44 | pages= 270-273
| doi = 10.1016/j.jpba.2007.01.052}}</ref> It is marketed as '''Uvasorb HEB''' by 3V Sigma.
