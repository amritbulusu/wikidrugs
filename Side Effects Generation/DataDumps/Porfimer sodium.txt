'''Porfimer sodium''', sold as '''Photofrin''', is a [[photosensitizer]] used in [[photodynamic therapy]] and [[radiation therapy]] and for [[palliative care|palliative treatment]] of obstructing endobronchial [[non-small cell lung carcinoma]] and obstructing [[esophageal cancer]].

Porfimer is a mixture of [[oligomers]] formed by ether and ester linkages of up to eight [[porphyrin]] units.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/drugsatfda_docs/label/2008/020451s019lbl.pdf |title=Porfimer injection Prescribing information}}</ref>  In practice, a red light source emitting at 630&nbsp;nm is used to excite the Porfimer oligomers.<ref name=LC>{{cite journal |url=http://journals.lww.com/jto/Fulltext/2006/06000/Photodynamic_Therapy__PDT__for_Lung_Cancers.18.aspx |title=Photodynamic Therapy (PDT) for Lung Cancers |year=2006 }}</ref>

Porfimer is [[Haematoporphyrin]] Derivative (HpD) (See [[Photodynamic therapy#20th century development of PDT|PDT]]).

==Approvals and indications==
It was approved in Canada in 1993 for the treatment of [[bladder cancer]].<ref name=LC/>
It was approved in Japan in 1994 (for early stage lung cancer?).<ref name=LC/>
It was approved by the [[U.S. Food and Drug Administration|U.S. FDA]] in December 1995 for [[esophageal cancer]], and in 1998, it was approved for the treatment of early [[non-small cell lung cancer]].<ref name=LC/>

In August 2003 the FDA approved its use for [[Barrett's esophagus]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/psn/printer-full.cfm?id=24 |title=FDA Patient Safety News: Show #20, October 2003 |date=October 2003 |accessdate=2009-08-17}}</ref>
