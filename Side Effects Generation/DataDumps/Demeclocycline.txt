'''Demeclocycline''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]) (brand names '''Declomycin''', '''Declostatin''', '''Ledermycin''', '''Bioterciclin''', '''Deganol''', '''Deteclo'''), also known under the brand names '''Detravis''', '''Meciclin''', '''Mexocine''', '''Clortetrin''', is a [[semisynthetic]] [[tetracycline antibiotic]] which was derived from a strain of ''[[Streptomyces aureofaciens]]''.<ref>{{DorlandsDict|three/000028168|demeclocycline}}</ref><ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA356|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=356–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA92|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=92–}}</ref>

==Uses==
It is officially indicated for the treatment of various types of [[bacteria]]l [[infection]]s.<ref name="urlDailyMed: About DailyMed">{{cite web |url=http://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?id=673 |title=DailyMed: About DailyMed |format= |work= |accessdate=2008-12-20}}</ref> It is used as an antibiotic in the treatment of [[Lyme disease]], [[Acne vulgaris|acne]], and [[bronchitis]].{{Citation needed|date=December 2008}}  Resistance, though, is gradually becoming more common, and demeclocycline is now rarely used for infections.

It is widely used (though [[off-label use|off-label]] in many countries) in the treatment of [[hyponatremia]] (low blood [[sodium]] concentration) due to the [[syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone]] (SIADH) when fluid restriction alone has been ineffective.<ref name=Goh>{{cite journal |author=Goh KP |title=Management of hyponatremia |journal=[[American Family Physician]] |volume=69 |issue=10 |pages=2387–94 |date=May 2004 |pmid=15168958 |url=http://www.aafp.org/afp/20040515/2387.html}}</ref> Physiologically, this works by reducing the responsiveness of the collecting tubule cells to ADH.

The use in SIADH actually relies on a side effect; demeclocycline induces [[diabetes insipidus|nephrogenic diabetes insipidus]] (dehydration due to the inability to concentrate urine).<ref name=Goh/> The use of demeclocycline in SIADH was first reported in 1975,<ref name="Cherrill">{{cite journal |vauthors=Cherrill DA, Stote RM, Birge JR, Singer I |title=Demeclocycline treatment in the syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone secretion |journal=[[Annals of Internal Medicine]] |volume=83 |issue=5 |pages=654–6 |date=November 1975 |pmid=173218 |doi=10.7326/0003-4819-83-5-654}}</ref> and, in 1978, a larger study found it to be more effective and better tolerated than [[lithium carbonate]], the only available treatment at the time.<ref name=Tolstoi>{{cite journal |author=Tolstoi LG |title=A brief review of drug-induced syndrome of inappropriate secretion of antidiuretic hormone |journal=[[Medscape|Medscape Pharmacotherapy]] |volume=4 |issue=1 |year=2002 |url=http://www.medscape.com/viewarticle/420687_print}} Retrieved on October 27, 2008.</ref> Demeclocycline has since been the drug of choice for treating SIADH, although it may be superseded as [[vasopressin receptor antagonist]]s, such as [[tolvaptan]], become available.<ref name=Tolstoi/>

==Contraindications==
Like [[tetracycline antibiotics|other tetracyclines]], demeclocycline is contraindicated in children and pregnant or nursing women. All members of this class interfere with [[bone]] development and may discolour [[tooth|teeth]].<ref name=Lexi-Comp>{{cite web |url=http://www.merck.com/mmpe/lexicomp/demeclocycline.html |title=Demeclocycline |date=August 2008 |author=Lexi-Comp |work=[[Merck Manual of Diagnosis and Therapy|The Merck Manual Professional]]}} Retrieved on October 27, 2008.</ref>

==Side effects and interactions==
These are similar to those of other tetracyclines. Skin reactions with sunlight have been reported.<ref name=Tolstoi/> Demeclocycline is unique in that it is the only tetracycline known to cause nephrogenic diabetes insipidus.

Tetracyclines bind to [[cation]]s, such as [[calcium]], [[iron]] (when given orally), and [[magnesium]], rendering them insoluble and inabsorbable for the gastrointestinal tract. Demeclocycline should not be taken with food (particularly milk and other dairy products) or [[antacid]]s.<ref name=Lexi-Comp/>

==Mechanism of action==
As with related tetracycline antibiotics, demeclocycline acts by binding to the [[30S]] ribosomal subunit to inhibit binding of aminoacyl tRNA which impairs [[protein synthesis]] by bacteria. It is [[bacteriostatic]] (it impairs bacterial growth, but does not kill bacteria directly).

Demeclocycline inhibits the [[renal function|renal]] action of [[antidiuretic hormone]] by interfering with the [[intracellular]] [[second messenger cascade]] (specifically, inhibiting [[adenylyl cyclase]] activation) after the hormone binds to [[Arginine vasopressin receptor 2|vasopressin V<sub>2</sub> receptor]]s in the [[kidney]].<ref name="FliersKorbonits2014">{{cite book|author1=Eric Fliers|author2=Marta Korbonits|author3=J.A. Romijn|title=Clinical Neuroendocrinology|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qH9zAwAAQBAJ&pg=PA43|date=28 August 2014|publisher=Elsevier Science|isbn=978-0-444-62612-7|pages=43–}}</ref><ref name="KovácsLichardus2012">{{cite book|author1=L. Kovács|author2=B. Lichardus|title=Vasopressin: Disturbed Secretion and Its Effects|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=UQztCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA180|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-009-0449-1|pages=180–}}</ref><ref name="SinghWilliams2009">{{cite book|author1=Ajay K. Singh|author2=Gordon H. Williams|title=Textbook of Nephro-Endocrinology|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TsbV_KMn4yAC&pg=PA251|date=12 January 2009|publisher=Academic Press|isbn=978-0-08-092046-7|pages=251–}}</ref> Exactly how demeclocycline does this has yet to be elucidated, however.<ref name="SinghWilliams2009" />
