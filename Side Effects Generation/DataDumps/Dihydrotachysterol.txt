'''Dihydrotachysterol''' (DHT) is a synthetic [[vitamin D]] analog activated in the [[liver]] that does not require [[renal]] hydroxylation like vitamin D<sub>2</sub> ([[ergocalciferol]]) and vitamin D<sub>3</sub> ([[cholecalciferol]]). DHT has a rapid onset of action (2 hours), a shorter half-life, and a greater effect on [[Mineralization (biology)|mineralization]] of bone salts than does vitamin D.<ref>{{cite journal |title = Comparison of Dihydrotachysterol and 5,6-trans Vitamin D3 on Intestinal Calcium Absorption in Patients with Chronic Renal Failure |author1=R.
