'''Acetohexamide''' (trade name '''Dymelor''') is a first-generation [[sulfonylurea]] [[medication]] used to treat [[diabetes mellitus type 2]], particularly in people whose diabetes cannot be controlled by diet alone.

==Mechanism of action==
Acetohexamide lowers blood sugar by stimulating the [[pancreas]] to secrete insulin and helping the body use [[insulin]] efficiently.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metforminsideeffects.biz |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-10-26 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111105044032/http://www.metforminsideeffects.biz/ |archivedate=2011-11-05 |df= }} – Metformin Side Effects</ref> The pancreas must produce insulin for this medication to work.  For this reason, acetohexamide is not used to treat [[diabetes mellitus type 1]].

==Risks==
Oral hypoglycemic drugs, including acetohexamide, have been associated with increased [[cardiovascular]] mortality. Talk to your doctor about the possible risks, benefits, and alternatives of using this drug for your condition.<ref>[http://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/druginfo/medmaster/a682478.html Medline Plus] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050911024428/http://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/druginfo/medmaster/a682478.html |date=September 11, 2005 }} – Acetohexamide</ref>
