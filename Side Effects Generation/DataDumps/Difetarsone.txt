'''Difetarsone''' is an [[antiprotozoal agent]]. Various studies have shown it to be particularly effective against ''[[Trichuris trichiura]]'', commonly known as the whipworm. Prior to the drugs use in the early 1970s, there were few effective treatments for this infection.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Difetarsone in Difetarsone in outpatient treatment of Trichuris trichiura infestation |author1=P.M. Leary |author2=C. Jones |author3=F. Douglas |author4=S.T. Boyd |date=June 1972 |pmc=1648795 |journal=Archives of Disease in Childhood  |pmid=4851370 |volume=49 |issue=6 |pages=486â€“8|doi=10.1136/adc.49.6.486 }}</ref> It has also been used to treat [[Entamoeba histolytica]] infections.<ref name=meyler>{{cite book |title=Meyler's Side Effects of Antimicrobial Drugs |first=Jeffrey K. |last=Aronson |authorlink=Jeffrey Aronson|page=834 |date=2009 |isbn=9780080932934 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Zu2s5QBqiEkC&pg=PT2728&lpg=PT2728&dq=Difetarsone+-wikipedia&source=bl&ots=F5zD-H7GwR&sig=PEyBvXHDvXuTBtNW-1lu0Or4RZE&hl=en&sa=X&ei=P1tTUv3PGeKdigLp7oH4DQ&ved=0CD8Q6AEwBDgK#v=onepage&q=Difetarsone%20-wikipedia&f=false |accessdate=October 7, 2013}}</ref>

Difetarsone often has minor side effects, which include rashes, nausea and vomiting. It has also resulted in [[angioedema]] in at least one known case.<ref name=meyler/>
