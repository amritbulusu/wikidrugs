'''Ethinamate''' ('''Valamin''', '''Valmid''') is a short-acting [[carbamate]]-derivative [[sedative-hypnotic]] [[medication]] used to treat [[insomnia]].  Regular use leads to [[drug tolerance]], and it is usually not effective for more than 7 days.  Prolonged use can lead to [[Chemical dependency|dependency]].

Ethinamate has been replaced by other medicines (particularly [[benzodiazepine]]s), and it is not available in the [[Netherlands]], the [[United States]] or [[Canada]].

==Chemistry==
Ethinamate (1-ethynylcyclohexanone carbamate) is synthesized by combining [[acetylene]] with [[cyclohexanone]] and then transforming the resulting [[methanol]] into a [[carbamate]] by the subsequent reaction with [[phosgene]], and later with [[ammonia]]. Some lithium metal or similar is used to make the acetylene react with the cyclohexanone in the first step.<ref>H. Pfeiffer, K. Junkman, {{US Patent|2816910}} (1957)</ref><ref>H. Emde, W. Grimme, {{Cite patent|DE|1021843}} (1953)</ref>
