'''Inositol nicotinate''' is a [[vasodilator]].

Inositol Nicotinate is given the term [[Niacin#Inositol hexanicotinate|Inositol hexaniacinate]], as well as "No-Flush Niacin".<ref name="ref1" />  [[Flush (physiology)|"Flushing"]], in physiology, is a broad term that essentially means that epidermal tissues have become reddened, such as when the skin is irritated from the histamine responses related to an allergic reaction, or from recent physical exercise, or even from anger or embarrassment. The reaction has been related to well over 200 causes.<ref name="ref2">Page Content attributed to webmaster and various authors. [http://www.wrongdiagnosis.com/symptoms/flushing/causes.htm "Causes of Flushing"] Wrongdiagnosis.com. Retrieved 2010-09-14. OP: Not applicable.</ref> This form of niacin is supposed to reduce or prevent flushing by being broken down into the metabolites [[Niacin]] (aka Nicotinic acid) and [[Inositol]] at a slower rate.<ref name="ref1">Reza Taheri, PharmD [http://www.medscape.com/viewarticle/447528 "No-Flush Niacin for the Treatment of Hyperlipidemia"] Medscape.com. Retrieved 2010-09-14. Originally Posted 2003-01-15. </ref>
