'''Estrazinol''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), or as '''estrazinol hydrobromide''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]) (developmental code name '''W-4454A'''), also known as '''3-methoxy-8-aza-19-norpregna-1,3,5(10)-trien-20-yn-17-ol''', is a [[Organic compound#Synthetic compounds|synthetic]], [[steroid]]al [[estrogen]] that was synthesized in 1968 and further characterized in 1973 but was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA503|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=503}}</ref> It is described as a [[solubility|water-soluble]] estrogen.<ref name="pmid4368700">{{cite journal | vauthors = Rassaert CL, DiPasquale G, Giannina T, Manning JP, Meli A | title = Estrogenic profile on a water-soluble estrogen, estrazinol hydrobromide | journal = Steroids Lipids Res | volume = 4 | issue = 5 | pages = 333â€“40 | year = 1973 | pmid = 4368700 | doi = | url = }}</ref>
