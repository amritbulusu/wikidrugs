'''Aceburic acid''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known as '''4-acetoxybutanoic acid''' or '''4-hydroxybutyric acid acetate''', is [[drug]] described as an [[analgesic]] which was never marketed.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&pg=PA1052|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=1052–}}</ref> It is the [[acetyl]] [[ester]] of [[gamma-hydroxybutyrate]] (GHB, which is 4-hydroxybutanoic acid),<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996" /> and based on its structural relation to GHB, is likely to behave as a [[prodrug]] to it.{{Citation needed|date=November 2014}}
