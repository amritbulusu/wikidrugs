'''Cetrimide''' is an [[antiseptic]] which is a mixture of different quaternary ammonium salts including [[cetrimonium bromide]] (CTAB).<ref name= AntibacterialActivity >{{cite journal |last= Ito.|first= Emma |title=Editorial |journal= Potential Use of Cetrimonium Bromide as an Apoptosis-Promoting Anticancer Agent for Head and Neck Cancer |date=November 2009 |volume= 76 |issue= 5 |pages= 969â€“983|url= http://molpharm.aspetjournals.org/content/76/5/969.long|accessdate= November 2009}}</ref> It was first discovered and developed by [[Imperial Chemical Industries|ICI]] and introduced under the brand name ''Cetavlon''.<ref>http://newspapers.nl.sg/Digitised/Article/straitstimes19721231.2.68.1.aspx</ref> It is used as a 1-3 % solution for cleaning roadside accident wounds.<ref>{{cite book |title=Essentials of Medical Pharmacology |edition=7th |first1=KD |last1=Tripathi |page=900}}</ref> Another antiseptic [[chlorhexidine]] was also discovered and first developed by [[Imperial Chemical Industries|ICI]]. [[Imperial Chemical Industries|ICI]] also introduced [[Savlon]] which was a combination of cetrimide and chlorhexidine. [[Imperial Chemical Industries|ICI]] later sold the [[Savlon]] brand OTC to [[Johnson & Johnson]] in May 1992.<ref>http://www.thefreelibrary.com/JOHNSON+%26+JOHNSON+ACQUIRES+SAVLON+OTC+BRANDS+FROM+ICI-a012169404</ref>

==Alcohol Denaturant==
Cetrimide can also be used as an alcohol denaturant to make it difficult to drink. Presumably, it has negative effects upon the digestive system or tastes bad {{citation needed|date=May 2016}}.
