'''Sulfalene''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]) or '''sulfametopyrazine''' ([[British Approved Name|BAN]]) is a long-acting [[sulfonamide (medicine)|sulfonamide]] [[antibacterial]] used for the treatment of [[chronic bronchitis]], [[urinary tract infection]]s and [[malaria]].<ref name="DrugBank">{{DrugBank|DB00664}}</ref><ref name = MD/> As of 2014 there were only two countries in which it is currently still marketed: [[Thailand]] and [[Ireland]].<ref name = MD>{{cite web|title=Sulfametopyrazine|work=Martindale: The Complete Drug Reference|publisher=Pharmaceutical Press|date=9 May 2013|accessdate=28 March 2014|editor=Brayfield, A|url=http://www.medicinescomplete.com/mc/martindale/current/4920-k.htm}}</ref> 

It was discovered by researchers at [[Farmitalia]] and first published in 1960 and was marketed as Kelfizina.<ref>Baruffa G. Clinical trials in Plasmodium falciparum malaria with a long-acting sulphonamide.
