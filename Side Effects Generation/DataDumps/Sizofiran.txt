'''Sizofiran''' is a [[beta-glucan]], also known as [[schizophyllan]] and abbreviated as SPG in many papers.  It is a [[polysaccharide]] consisting of repeating β(1→3)-linked <small>D</small>-glucose units with occasional β(1→6)-branches.<ref name="Kony">{{cite journal | journal = Biophys J | year = 2007 | volume = 93 | issue = 2 | pages = 442–455 | doi = 10.1529/biophysj.106.086116 | title = Explicit-Solvent Molecular Dynamics Simulations of the Polysaccharide Schizophyllan in Water |author1=David B. Kony |author2=Wolfgang Damm |author3=Serge Stoll |author4=Wilfred F. van Gunsteren |author5=Philippe H. Hünenberger  |last-author-amp=yes | pmid = 17237195 | pmc = 1896245}}</ref>  This generates a very stiff triple-helical structure in water.<ref name="Kony"/>
