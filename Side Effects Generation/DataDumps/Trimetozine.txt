'''Trimetozine''' ('''Opalene''', '''Trimolide''', '''Trioxazine''') is a [[sedative]] that has been marketed in [[Europe]] since 1959.<ref name="isbn3-88763-075-0">{{cite book | author = Swiss Pharmaceutical Society | title = Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory (Book with CD-ROM) | publisher = Medpharm Scientific Publishers | location = Boca Raton | year = 2000 | pages = 1932 | isbn = 3-88763-075-0 | oclc = | doi = | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&lpg=PA1067&dq=trimetozine&pg=PA1067#v=onepage&q=trimetozine&f=false}}</ref><ref name="isbn0-412-46630-9">{{cite book | author = David J. Triggle | title = Dictionary of pharmacological agents | publisher = Chapman & Hall | location = London | year = 1997 | pages = | isbn = 0-412-46630-9 | oclc = | doi = | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&lpg=PA2065&dq=trimetozine&pg=PA2065#v=onepage&q=trimetozine&f=false}}</ref> It also has mild [[minor tranquilizer|tranquilizing]] effects and has been used in the treatment of [[anxiety]].<ref name="pmid5425739">{{cite journal |vauthors=Taverna P, Ferrari G | title = [Clinical trial of a new tranquilizing agent: trioxazine] | language = Italian | journal = Minerva Medica | volume = 61 | issue = 46 | pages = 2574–90 |date=June 1970 | pmid = 5425739 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid5730033">{{cite journal |author1=Shpak VM |author2=Shcheglova AIu | title = [Trioxazine in the treatment of night anxiety in children] | language = Russian | journal = Pediatriia | volume = 47 | issue = 8 | pages = 76–7 |date=August 1968 | pmid = 5730033 | doi = | url = }}</ref> Its [[mechanism of action]] is unclear.{{Citation needed|date=August 2010}}

Conversion of the amide in trimetozine to the [[thioamide]] gives [[Tritiozine]].
