'''Dimetindene'''<ref>https://www.wikigenes.org/e/chem/e/21855.html</ref> (trade name [[Fenistil]]; other name '''dimethindene maleate''') is an antiallergic drug. Dimetindene is an [[antihistamine]]/[[anticholinergic]] that is a selective [[H1 antagonist]].<ref>https://www.drugs.com/international/dimetindene.html</ref> As dimetindene only minimally passes across the blood-brain barrier,<ref>https://www.wikigenes.org/e/ref/e/1353628.html</ref> it is classified as a second generation [[antihistamine]].

== Clinical use ==
Dimetindene is used orally and topically as an [[antipruritic]].<ref>{{Cite journal | last1 = Wexler | first1 = L. | title = A Clinical Evaluation of Dimethindene | journal = Current Therapeutic Research, Clinical and Experimental | volume = 4 | pages = 306â€“9 | year = 1962 | pmid = 14006402}}</ref>  
It is used topically to treat skin irritations, such as insect bites. Dimentidene is also administered orally to treat allergies, such as hay fever.  Unlike first generation antihistamines, it causes only minimal drowsiness.
