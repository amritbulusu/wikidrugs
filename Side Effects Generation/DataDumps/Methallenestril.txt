'''Methallenestril''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (brand names '''Cur-men''', '''Ercostrol''', '''Geklimon''', '''Novestrine''', '''Vallestril'''), also known as '''methallenoestril''' ([[British Approved Name|BAN]]) and as '''methallenestrol''', as well as '''Horeau's acid''',<ref name="Heftmann1970">{{cite book|author=Erich Heftmann|title=Steroid Biochemistry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gAlrAAAAMAAJ|year=1970|publisher=Academic Press|page=144}}</ref><ref name="Dodds1949">{{cite journal|last1=Dodds|first1=E. C.|title=Synthetic aestrogens|journal=Journal of Pharmacy and Pharmacology|volume=1|issue=1|year=1949|pages=137–147|issn=0022-3573|doi=10.1111/j.2042-7158.1949.tb12391.x}}</ref> is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]], [[nonsteroidal]] [[estrogen]] and a [[chemical derivative|derivative]] of [[allenolic acid]] and [[allenestrol]] (specifically, a [[methyl group|methyl]] [[ether]] of it) that was formerly used to treat [[menstrual disorder|menstrual issues]] but is now no longer marketed.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&pg=PA1295|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=1295–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M.
