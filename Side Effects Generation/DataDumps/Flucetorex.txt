'''Flucetorex''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) is an [[substituted amphetamine|amphetamine]] which was investigated as an [[anorectic]] but does not appear to have ever been marketed.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Z_mfTTIApVEC&pg=PA568|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=568–}}</ref><ref name="Organization2000">{{cite book|author=World Health Organization|title=International Nonproprietary Names (INN) for Pharmaceutical Substances|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HcogPwAACAAJ|year=2000|publisher=World Health Organization|isbn=978-0-11-986227-0}}</ref> It is related to [[fenfluramine]].<ref name="Bray1980">{{cite book|author=George A. Bray|title=Obesity: comparative methods of weight control|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5OVrAAAAMAAJ|date=1 July 1980|publisher=Technomic|isbn=978-0-87762-261-1}}</ref><ref name="Sullivan1976">{{cite journal|last1=Sullivan|first1=Ann C.|title=Chapter 21: Agents for the Treatment of Obesity|volume=11|year=1976|pages=200–208|issn=0065-7743|doi=10.1016/S0065-7743(08)61404-9}}</ref>
