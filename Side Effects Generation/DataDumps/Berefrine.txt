'''Berefrine''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]), also known as '''burefrine''', is a [[sympathomimetic]] and [[mydriatic]] [[drug|agent]] that was never marketed.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Z_mfTTIApVEC&pg=PA615|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=615–}}</ref><ref name="Organization2000">{{cite book|author=World Health Organization|title=International Nonproprietary Names (INN) for Pharmaceutical Substances|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HcogPwAACAAJ|year=2000|publisher=World Health Organization|isbn=978-0-11-986227-0}}</ref> It is an [[oxazolidine]] [[prodrug]] of [[phenylephrine]], and hence, an [[Alpha-1 adrenergic receptor|α<sub>1</sub>-adrenergic receptor]] [[agonist]].[https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/compound/20055419#section=Synonyms]
