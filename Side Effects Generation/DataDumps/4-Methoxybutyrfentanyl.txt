'''4-Methoxybutyrfentanyl''' (also known as '''4-MeO-BF''') is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] that is an [[structural analog|analog]] of [[butyrfentanyl]] and has been sold online as a [[designer drug]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.caymanchem.com/app/template/Product.vm/catalog/18089 | title=4-MeO-Butyrfentanyl | publisher=Cayman Chemical}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.nsddb.eu/substance/523/ | title=4-Methoxybutyrfentanyl | publisher=New Synthetic Drugs Database}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.policija.si/apps/nfl_response_web/0_Analytical_Reports_final/4-MEO-BF-ID-1263-15-report_final.pdf | title=ANALYTICAL REPORT - 4-MeO-BF | publisher=European project RESPONSE}}</ref>

== Side effects ==

Side effects of [[fentanyl]] analogs are similar to those of fentanyl itself, which include [[itching]], [[nausea]] and potentially serious [[respiratory depression]], which can be life-threatening. Fentanyl analogs have killed hundreds of people throughout Europe and the former Soviet republics since the most recent resurgence in use began in Estonia in the early 2000s, and novel derivatives continue to appear.<ref>{{cite journal | url=http://www.ijdp.org/article/S0955-3959%2815%2900097-3/abstract | title=Fentanyls: Are we missing the signs? Highly potent and on the rise in Europe. | author=Jane Mounteney | author2=Isabelle Giraudon | author3=Gleb Denissov | author4=Paul Griffiths | journal=International Journal of Drug Policy | date=July 2015 | volume=26 | issue=7 | pages=626–631 |doi=10.1016/j.drugpo.2015.04.003 | pmid=25976511}}</ref>

Life-threatening adverse reactions have been observed.<ref>{{cite journal | author1=Anders Helander | url=http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.3109/15563650.2016.1139715 | title=Intoxications involving the fentanyl analogs acetylfentanyl, 4-methoxybutyrfentanyl and furanylfentanyl: results from the Swedish STRIDA project | date=April 2016 | author2=Matilda Bäckberg | author3=Olof Beck | journal=Clinical Toxicology | volume=54 | issue=4 | pages=324–332 | doi=10.3109/15563650.2016.1139715 | pmid=26850293}}</ref>

== Legal status ==

4-Methoxybutyrfentanyl is illegal in Sweden as of 26. January 2016.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.folkhalsomyndigheten.se/nyheter-och-press/nyhetsarkiv/2015/november/31-nya-amnen-kan-klassas-som-narkotika-eller-halsofarlig-vara/ | title=31 nya ämnen kan klassas som narkotika eller hälsofarlig vara | publisher=Folkhälsomyndigheten | language=Swedish | date=November 2015}}</ref>
