'''Fletazepam'''<ref>{{Cite patent|DE|2106175}}</ref> is a drug which is a [[benzodiazepine]] derivative. It has [[sedative]] and [[anxiolytic]] effects similar to those produced by other benzodiazepine derivatives, but is mainly notable for its strong [[muscle relaxant]] properties.<ref>{{Cite journal | last1 = Barnett | first1 = A. | last2 = Goldstein | first2 = J. | last3 = Fiedler | first3 = EP. | last4 = Taber | first4 = RI. | title = The pharmacology of fletazepam a centrally-acting muscle relaxant. | journal = Arch Int Pharmacodyn Ther | volume = 212 | issue = 1 | pages = 164–74 |date=Nov 1974 | PMID = 4476200 }}</ref>

Fletazepam is most closely related to other ''N''-trifluoroethyl substituted benzodiazepines such as [[halazepam]] and [[quazepam]].<ref>{{Cite journal | last1 = Iorio | first1 = LC. | last2 = Barnett | first2 = A. | last3 = Billard | first3 = W. | title = Selective affinity of 1-N-trifluoroethyl benzodiazepines for cerebellar type 1 receptor sites. | journal = Life Sci | volume = 35 | issue = 1 | pages = 105–13 |date=Jul 1984 | pmid = 6738302 | doi=10.1016/0024-3205(84)90157-7}}</ref>
