'''JWH-249''' ('''1-pentyl-3-(2-bromophenylacetyl)indole''') is a [[synthetic cannabinoid]] from the [[phenylacetylindole]] family, which acts as a [[cannabinoid agonist]] with about 2.4x selectivity for [[Cannabinoid receptor 1|CB<sub>1</sub>]] with a [[Dissociation constant|K<sub>i</sub>]] of 8.4nM ± 1.8 and 20nM ± 2 at [[Cannabinoid receptor 2|CB<sub>2</sub>]]. Similar to the related 2'-[[methoxy]] compound [[JWH-250]], the 2'-[[chloro]] compound [[JWH-203]], and the 2'-[[methyl]] compound [[JWH-251]], JWH-249 has a [[phenyl]][[acetyl]] group in place of the [[naphthoyl]] ring used in most [[aminoalkylindole]] [[cannabinoid]] compounds.
