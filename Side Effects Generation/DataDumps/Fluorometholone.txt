'''Fluorometholone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]], [[Japanese Accepted Name|JAN]]) (brand names '''Efflumidex''', '''Flucon''', '''FML Forte''', '''FML''', others), also known as '''6α-methyl-9α-fluoro-11β,17α-dihydroxypregna-1,4-diene-3,20-dione''', is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]] [[glucocorticoid]] which is used in the treatment of [[inflammation|inflammatory]] [[eye disease]]s.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA566|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=566–}}</ref><ref name="IndexNominum2000">{{cite book|title=Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA458|date=January 2000|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-3-88763-075-1|pages=458–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA123|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=123–}}</ref> The C17α [[acetate]] [[ester]], [[fluorometholone acetate]] (brand name Flarex), is also a glucocorticoid and is used for similar indications.<ref name="Elks2014" /><ref name="IndexNominum2000" /><ref name="MortonHall2012" /> Specifically, it is indicated for use in the treatment of steroid-responsive inflammatory conditions of the palpebral and bulbar conjunctiva, cornea, and anterior segment of the eye.{{Citation needed|date=December 2016}}

Most common indication for this drug is seasonal allergic conjunctivitis and other allergies of the eye.
