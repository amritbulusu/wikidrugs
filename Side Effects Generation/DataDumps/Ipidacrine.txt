'''Ipidacrine''' ('''Neiromidin''') is a drug first synthesized by the National Research Center for Biologically Active Compounds in the [[Russian Federation]]. This compound is a [[derivative (chemistry)|derivative]] of [[4-aminopyridine]] and is structurally similar to [[tacrine]].<ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1111/j.1527-3458.1998.tb00067.x | title = Ipidacrine (NIK-247): A Review of Multiple Mechanisms as an Antidementia Agent | year = 1998 | last1 = Kojima | first1 = Jun | last2 = Onodera | first2 = Kenji | last3 = Ozeki | first3 = Mitsuo | last4 = Nakayama | first4 = Kunio | journal = CNS Drug Reviews | volume = 4 | issue = 3 | pages = 247}}</ref>

Ipidacrine is a reversible [[acetylcholinesterase inhibitor]] used in memory disorders of different origins.<ref>{{cite journal | pmid = 21350422 | year = 2011 | last1 = Damulin | first1 = IV | last2 = Stepkina | first2 = DA | last3 = Lokshina | first3 = AB | title = Neuromidin in mixed vascular and Alzheimer's dementia | volume = 111 | issue = 2 | pages = 40–3 | journal = Zhurnal nevrologii i psikhiatrii imeni S.S. Korsakova / Ministerstvo zdravookhraneniia i meditsinskoi promyshlennosti Rossiiskoi Federatsii, Vserossiiskoe obshchestvo nevrologov \i&#93; Vserossiiskoe obshchestvo psikhiatrov}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | pmid = 23120773 | year = 2011 | last1 = Pustokhanova | first1 = LV | last2 = Morozova | first2 = EM | title = Cognitive disorders in the early rehabilitation period of ischemic stroke and possibilities of their treatment with neuromidin | volume = 111 | issue = 4 Pt 2 | pages = 23–7 | journal = Zhurnal nevrologii i psikhiatrii imeni S.S. Korsakova / Ministerstvo zdravookhraneniia i meditsinskoi promyshlennosti Rossiiskoi Federatsii, Vserossiiskoe obshchestvo nevrologov \i&#93; Vserossiiskoe obshchestvo psikhiatrov}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | pmid = 9656230 | year = 1998 | last1 = Onodera | first1 = K | last2 = Kojima | first2 = J | last3 = Wachi | first3 = M | title = Ipidacrine (NIK-247), a novel antidementia, rapidly enters the brain and improves scopolamine-induced amnesia in rats during the Morris water maze task | volume = 18 | issue = 2 | pages = 33–7 | journal = Nihon shinkei seishin yakurigaku zasshi = Japanese journal of psychopharmacology}}</ref>

Ipidacrine directly stimulates impulse transmission in the [[central nervous system]] and neuromuscular synapses by blocking membrane [[potassium channel]]s. Ipidacrine enhances not only [[choline]], but also [[adrenaline]], [[serotonin]], [[histamine]], and [[oxytocin]] effects on smooth muscle.<ref>[http://olainfarm.lv/en/prescription-drugs/neiromidin-3 Neiromidin Product Information]</ref>
