'''Dimepregnen''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]]) (developmental code name '''ST-1411'''), or '''6α,16α-dimethylpregn-4-en-3β-ol-20-one''', is a [[pregnene]] [[steroid]] described as an [[antiestrogen]] that was synthesized in 1968 and was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA428|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=428–}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal| pmc=1699868 | pmid=5440553 | volume=1 | title=Report of the Special Committee on Organ Transplantation | journal=Br Med J | pages=750–1}}</ref> It is similar in [[chemical structure|structure]] to the [[progestin]]s and [[progesterone]] derivatives [[melengestrol]] and [[anagestone]].
