'''Cilofungin''' ([[International nonproprietary name|INN]])<ref name="INN" /> is the first clinically applied member of the [[echinocandin]] family of [[antifungal]] drugs. It was derived from a fungus in the genus ''[[Aspergillus]]''. It accomplishes this by interfering with an invading fungus' ability to synthesize the cell wall (specifically, it inhibits the synthesis of [[Beta-glucan|(1→3)-β-<small>D</small>-glucan]]).<ref>{{cite book
