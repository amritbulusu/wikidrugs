'''''p''-Chlorocresol''' (''p''-chloro-''m''-cresol; PCMC; brand name: Preventol CMK) is a [[chlorinated]] [[phenol]] which is used as an [[antiseptic]] and [[preservative]].<ref>{{citation|title=Handbook of Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Excipients|pages=87|author=Susan C Smolinske|year=1992}}</ref>  It forms colourless, dimorphous [[crystal]]s at room temperature and is only slightly soluble in water.  For use as a [[disinfectant]] such as a hand wash, it is commonly dissolved in alcohol in combination with other phenols.<ref>{{citation|title=Hospital Epidemiology and Infection Control|pages=1741|author=C. Glen Mayhall|year=2004}}</ref>  It is a moderate [[allergen]] for sensitive [[skin]].<ref>{{citation|title=Toxicology of Skin|pages=339|author=Howard I. Maibach|year=2001}}</ref>
