'''Methyl-1-testosterone''' (developmental code name '''SC-11195'''), also known as '''17α-methyl-4,5α-dihydro-δ<sup>1</sup>-testosterone (17α-methyl-δ<sup>1</sup>-DHT)''' or '''17α-methyl-5α-androst-1-en-17β-ol-3-one''', as well as '''methyldihydroboldenone''', is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]], [[oral administration|orally active]] [[anabolic-androgenic steroid]] (AAS) that was never marketed. It is the [[17α-alkylated anabolic steroid|17α-methyl]] derivative of [[1-testosterone]] (Δ<sup>1</sup>-DHT; dihydroboldenone).

Methyl-1-testosterone is on the [[World Anti-Doping Agency]]'s list of prohibited substances,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wada-ama.org/Documents/World_Anti-Doping_Program/WADP-Prohibited-list/2012/WADA_Prohibited_List_2012_EN.pdf |title=The World Anti-Doping Code: The 2012 Prohibited List |publisher=[[World Anti-Doping Agency]] |accessdate=2012-07-17 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120513020202/http://www.wada-ama.org/Documents/World_Anti-Doping_Program/WADP-Prohibited-list/2012/WADA_Prohibited_List_2012_EN.pdf |archivedate=2012-05-13 |df= }}</ref> and is therefore banned from use in most major sports.
