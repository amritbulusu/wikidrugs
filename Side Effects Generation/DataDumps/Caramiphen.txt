'''Caramiphen''' is an [[anticholinergic]] drug used in the treatment of [[Parkinson's disease]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.drugs.com/international/caramiphen.html | publisher = drugs.com | title = Caramiphen | accessdate=26 September 2012}}</ref>  In combination with [[phenylpropanolamine]] it is used as a [[cough suppressant]] and [[nasal decongestant]] to treat symptoms associated with respiratory illnesses such as [[common cold|cold]], [[Allergy|allergies]], [[hay fever]], and [[sinusitis]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.medicinenet.com/caramiphen_with_phenylpropanolamine-oral/article.htm |title=Caramiphen with phenylpropanolamine-oral, Ordrine AT, Rescaps-D, Tuss Vernade, Tusso-Gest |publisher=medicine.net |accessdate=26 September 2012 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121029191620/http://www.medicinenet.com/caramiphen_with_phenylpropanolamine-oral/article.htm |archivedate=29 October 2012 |df= }}</ref>
