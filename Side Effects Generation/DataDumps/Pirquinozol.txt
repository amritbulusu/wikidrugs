'''Pirquinozol''' ('''SQ-13,847''') is a [[drug]] which was investigated as an anti[[allergy|allergen]] and anti[[asthma]]tic agent in the early 1980s but was never marketed.<ref name="pmid6162946">{{cite journal |vauthors=Free CA, Hall LE | title = Antiallergic properties of SQ 13,847, an orally effective agent. II. Activity in vitro | journal = The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics | volume = 213 | issue = 3 | pages = 437–40 |date=June 1980 | pmid = 6162946 | doi = | url = http://jpet.aspetjournals.org/cgi/pmidlookup?view=long&pmid=6162946}}</ref><ref name="pmid7205610">{{cite journal |vauthors=Casey FB, Abboa-Offei BE, Marretta J | title = Antiallergic properties of SQ 13,847, an orally effective agent. I. Activity in vivo | journal = The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics | volume = 213 | issue = 3 | pages = 432–6 |date=June 1980 | pmid = 7205610 | doi = | url = http://jpet.aspetjournals.org/cgi/pmidlookup?view=long&pmid=7205610}}</ref><ref name="pmid6135784">{{cite journal |vauthors=Casey FB, Abboa-Offei BE | title = Anti-allergic properties of pirquinozol (SQ 13,847) an orally effective agent. Evaluation in an anti-IgE-induced pulmonary function model in rats | journal = The Journal of Pharmacy and Pharmacology | volume = 35 | issue = 6 | pages = 394–6 |date=June 1983 | pmid = 6135784 | doi = 10.1111/j.2042-7158.1983.tb02967.x| url = }}</ref> Notably, pirquinozol is not an [[antihistamine]], though it does block the release of [[histamine]] evoked by [[allergen]]s, and it does not bind to [[beta-adrenergic|β-adrenergic receptor]]s either.<ref name="pmid6162946"/>
