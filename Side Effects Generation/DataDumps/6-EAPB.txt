'''6-EAPB''' (1-(benzofuran-6-yl)-N-ethylpropan-2-amine) is a psychedelic and entactogenic drug of the [[benzofuran]] class;<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Taschwer|first1=Magdalena|last2=Hofer|first2=Manfred G.|last3=Schmid|first3=Martin G.|title=Enantioseparation of benzofurys and other novel psychoactive compounds by CE and sulfobutylether β-cyclodextrin as chiral selector added to the BGE|journal=ELECTROPHORESIS|date=October 2014|volume=35|issue=19|pages=2793–2799|doi=10.1002/elps.201400164}}</ref> it is structurally related to [[6-APB]] and [[MDMA]].

==Legality==
As an N-ethyl derivative of 6-APB, 6-EAPB fell outside the scope of the Temporary Class Drug ban issued by the Home Office on June 10, 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/temporary-class-drug-order-report-on-benzofury-and-nbome-compounds | title=Temporary class drug order on benzofury and NBOMe compounds | accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref>
The ACMD has advised that 6-EAPB (and other benzofurans) are moved to Class B,<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/acmd-recommends-permanent-ban-on-two-legal-highs | title=ACMD recommends permanent ban on two "legal highs" | accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref> this came into action on 10 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/ban-on-nbome-and-benzofurans-comes-into-force | title=Ban on NBOMe and benzofurans comes into force | accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref>
