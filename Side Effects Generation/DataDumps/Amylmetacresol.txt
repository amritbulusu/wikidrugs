'''Amylmetacresol''' ('''AMC''') is an [[antiseptic]] used to treat [[infection]]s of the [[mouth]] and [[throat]]. It is used as an [[active pharmaceutical ingredient]] in [[Strepsils]], [[Cēpacol]], [[Gorpils]] and [[Lorsept]] [[throat lozenges]].

Chemically, AMC is derivative of [[m-Cresol|''m''-cresol]], with a [[pentyl]] group attached to the sixth carbon atom. The pure substance melts at 24&nbsp;°C, and boils between 137 to 139&nbsp;°C (at 6.7 kPa). It is soluble in water, ethanol, acetone, ether, and oil. AMC exhibits low toxicity, and a [[Rideal-Walker coefficient|RW coefficient]] of 250.<ref>{{cite journal | last=Twort | first=CC | author2=Baker, AH | year = 1942 | title=Further Researches on Bactericidal Mists and Smokes | journal=The Journal of Hygiene | pages=266–283 | volume=42 | issue=3 | pmc=2199819 | pmid=20475630 | doi=10.1017/s0022172400035476}}</ref>
