'''Setastine''' ('''Loderix''') is an [[antihistamine]] used to treat [[allergies]] and [[rhinitis]].<ref name="pmid1677500">{{cite journal |author1=Lantos A |author2=Tóth A |author3=Zsiray M | title = Loderix (setastine) tablets in the treatment of allergic rhinoconjunctivitis | journal = Therapia Hungarica (English Edition) | volume = 39 | issue = 1 | pages = 22–4 | year = 1991 | pmid = 1677500 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid2576575">{{cite journal |author1=Hirschberg A |author2=Ribári O |author3=Krasznai M | title = Results obtained with Loderix tablet in chronic rhinitis patients | journal = Therapia Hungarica (English Edition) | volume = 37 | issue = 4 | pages = 202–4 | year = 1989 | pmid = 2576575 | doi = | url = }}</ref>

== Pharmacology ==

=== Pharmacodynamics ===

Setastine acts as a highly [[functional selectivity|selective]] [[H1 receptor|H<sub>1</sub> receptor]] [[receptor antagonist|antagonist]].<ref name="pmid1982751">{{cite journal  |vauthors=Porszász J, Varga F, Porszász KG, etal | title = Pharmacology of the new H1-receptor antagonist setastine hydrochloride | journal = Arzneimittel-Forschung | volume = 40 | issue = 12 | pages = 1340–5 |date=December 1990 | pmid = 1982751 | doi = | url = }}</ref> It has no [[anticholinergic]], [[antiadrenergic]], or [[antiserotonergic]] effects.

=== Pharmacokinetics ===

Setastine penetrates the [[blood-brain-barrier]] only weakly, hence, it is not [[sedative|sedating]] or is only mildly so.<ref name="pmid1982751"/>
