'''Etilevodopa''' ('''TV-1203''') is a [[dopaminergic]] agent which was developed as a treatment for [[Parkinson's disease]].<ref>{{cite journal |title = Pharmacokinetics of Etilevodopa Compared to Levodopa in Patient's With Parkinson's Disease: An Open-label, Randomized, Crossover Study |author1=Djaldetti Ruth |author2=Giladi Nir |author3=Hassin-Baer Sharon |author4=Shabtai Hertzel |author5=Melamed Eldad |journal = Clinical Neuropharmacology |date=November–December 2003 |volume = 26 |issue = 6 |pages = 322–326 |url = http://journals.lww.com/clinicalneuropharm/Abstract/2003/11000/Pharmacokinetics_of_Etilevodopa_Compared_to.12.aspx |doi=10.1097/00002826-200311000-00012}}</ref> It is the [[ethyl group|ethyl]] [[ester]] of [[levodopa]]. It was never marketed.
