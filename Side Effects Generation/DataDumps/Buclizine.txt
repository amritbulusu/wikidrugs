'''Buclizine''' is an [[antihistamine]] and [[anticholinergic]] of the [[diphenylmethylpiperazine]] group. It is considered to be an [[antiemetic]], similar to [[meclizine]].<ref name="pmid22469258">{{cite journal | vauthors = Mostafa GA, Al-Badr AA | title = Buclizine | journal = Profiles Drug Subst Excip Relat Methodol | volume = 36 | issue = | pages = 1–33 | year = 2011 | pmid = 22469258 | doi = 10.1016/B978-0-12-387667-6.00001-4 | url = }}</ref>

In the [[United Kingdom]], buclizine is one of three drugs contained in [[Migraleve]] tablets, marketed by [[McNeil Laboratories|McNeil Healthcare]] at [[migraine]] sufferers.<ref name="pmid4070325">{{cite journal | vauthors = Behrendt WA, Cserepes J | title = Acute toxicity and analgesic action of a combination of buclizine, codeine and paracetamol ('Migraleve') in tablet and suppository form in rats | journal = Pharmatherapeutica | volume = 4 | issue = 5 | pages = 322–31 | year = 1985 | pmid = 4070325 | doi = | url = }}</ref>
