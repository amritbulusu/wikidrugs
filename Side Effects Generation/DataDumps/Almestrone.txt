'''Almestrone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (developmental code names '''Ba 38372''', '''Ciba 38372'''), also known as '''7α-methylestrone''', is a [[Organic compound#Synthetic compounds|synthetic]], [[steroid]]al [[estrogen]] that was synthesized in 1967 but was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA900|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=900–}}</ref> It is used as a [[precursor (chemistry)|precursor]] in the [[chemical synthesis|synthesis]] of several highly [[biological activity|active]] steroids.<ref name="KrauseAksin-Artok2011">{{cite journal|last1=Krause|first1=Norbert|last2=Aksin-Artok|first2=Özge|title=Copper-Mediated and Copper-Catalyzed Addition and Substitution Reactions of Extended Multiple Bond Systems|year=2011|doi=10.1002/9780470682531.pat0450}}</ref><ref name="Ribeiro MoraisYoshioka2006">{{cite journal|last1=Ribeiro Morais|first1=Goreti|last2=Yoshioka|first2=Naho|last3=Watanabe|first3=Masataka|last4=Mataka|first4=Shuntaro|last5=Neves Oliveira|first5=Cristina das|last6=Thiemann|first6=Thies|title=C7-Substituted Estranes and Related Steroids|journal=Mini-Reviews in Organic Chemistry|volume=3|issue=3|year=2006|pages=229–251|issn=1570-193X|doi=10.2174/1570193X10603030229}}</ref>
