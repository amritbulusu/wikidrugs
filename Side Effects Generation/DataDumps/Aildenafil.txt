'''Aildenafil''' (methisosildenafil) is a synthetic chemical compound that is a [[structural analog]] of [[sildenafil]] (Viagra).<ref>{{Cite journal |author1=Zhao, Xia |author2=Sun, Peihong |author3=Zhou, Ying |author4=Liu, Yuwang |author5=Zhang, Huilin |author6=Gu, Jingkai |author7=Cui, Yimin | title = Pharmacokinetics and safety of aildenafil tablets in healthy Chinese male subjects after multiple dose administration | journal = Zhongguo Linchuang Yaolixue Zazhi | year = 2009 | volume = 25 | issue = 2 | pages = 120–123}}</ref>  It was first reported in 2003,<ref>Liu, Baoshun.  Preparation of pyrazolopyrimidine derivatives for treatment of impotence. {{cite patent|WO|2003016313}}</ref> and it is not approved by any health regulation agency.  Like sildenafil, aildenafil is a [[PDE5 inhibitor|phosphodiesterase type 5 inhibitor]].

Aildenafil has been found as an adulterant in a variety of supplements which are sold as "natural" or "herbal" sexual enhancement products.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Gryniewicz | first1 = CM | last2 = Reepmeyer | first2 = JC | last3 = Kauffman | first3 = JF | last4 = Buhse | first4 = LF | title = Detection of undeclared erectile dysfunction drugs and analogues in dietary supplements by ion mobility spectrometry | journal = Journal of pharmaceutical and biomedical analysis | volume = 49 | issue = 3 | pages = 601–6 | year = 2009 | pmid = 19150190 | doi = 10.1016/j.jpba.2008.12.002 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author1=Choi, Dong Mi |author2=Park, Sangaeh |author3=Yoon, Tae Hyung |author4=Jeong, Hye Kyoung |author5=Pyo, Jae Sung |author6=Park, Janghyun |author7=Kim, Deukjoon |author8=Kwon, Sung Won | title = Determination of analogs of sildenafil and vardenafil in foods by column liquid chromatography with a photodiode array detector, mass spectrometry, and nuclear magnetic resonance spectrometry | journal = Journal of AOAC International | year = 2008 | volume = 91 | issue = 3 | pages = 580–588 | pmid = 18567304}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1016/j.jpba.2007.04.011 |author1=Reepmeyer, John C. |author2=Woodruff, Jeffrey T. | title = Use of liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry and a chemical cleavage reaction for the structure elucidation of a new sildenafil analogue detected as an adulterant in an herbal dietary supplement | journal = Journal of Pharmaceutical and Biomedical Analysis | year = 2007 | volume = 44 | issue = 4 | pages = 887–893 | pmid = 17532168}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1016/j.jpba.2006.11.037 |author1=Reepmeyer, John C. |author2=Woodruff, Jeffrey T. |author3='Avignon, D. Andre. | title = Structure elucidation of a novel analogue of sildenafil detected as an adulterant in an herbal dietary supplement | journal = Journal of Pharmaceutical and Biomedical Analysis  | year = 2007 | volume = 43 | issue = 5 | pages = 1615–1621 | pmid = 17207601}}</ref><ref>[http://www.fda.gov/Safety/Recalls/EnforcementReports/ucm217780.htm Enforcement Report for June 30, 2010], [[United States Food and Drug Administration]]</ref>  The [[United States Food and Drug Administration]] has warned consumers that any sexual enhancement product that claims to work as well as prescription products is likely to contain such a contaminant.<ref>[http://www.fda.gov/ForConsumers/ConsumerUpdates/ucm048386.htm Hidden Risks of Erectile Dysfunction "Treatments" Sold Online], [[United States Food and Drug Administration]], February 21, 2009</ref>
