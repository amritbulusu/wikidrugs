'''Iontocaine''' was an anesthetic medication, marketed under the two brand names Numby and Phoresor PM900 by IOMED inc.  It is a [[local anesthetic with vasoconstrictor]], administered via [[iontophoresis]] through the skin.  It can numb up to 10&nbsp;mm of skin in as little as 10 minutes.  It is a 2% [[lidocaine]], 0.01&nbsp;mg/ml [[epinephrine]] solution.  It was manufactured by IOMED, Inc. 

Iontocaine was approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration on 21 December 1995.<ref>[http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cder/drugsatfda/index.cfm United States Food and Drug Administration, Drug Approval Reports]</ref>  It was discontinued by the manufacturer in 2005.<ref>United States Food and Drug Administration, August 2005: Additions and Deletions to the Drug Product List. [http://www.fda.gov/Drugs/default.htm]</ref>
