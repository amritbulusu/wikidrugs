'''Perflubron''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]/[[United States Adopted Name|USAN]], or '''perfluorooctyl bromide'''; brand name '''Imagent''') is a [[contrast medium]] for [[magnetic resonance imaging]], [[computer tomography]] and [[sonography]].<ref>{{Cite journal 
| last1 = Mattrey | first1 = R. F. 
| title = Perfluorooctylbromide: A new contrast agent for CT, sonography, and MR imaging 
| journal = AJR. American journal of roentgenology 
| volume = 152 
| issue = 2 
| pages = 247–252 
| year = 1989 
| pmid = 2643258
 | doi=10.2214/ajr.152.2.247
}}</ref> It was approved for this use in the United States by the [[Food and Drug Administration]] in 1993.<ref>[http://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cder/drugsatfda/index.cfm?fuseaction=Search.DrugDetails FDA Approved Drug Products]</ref>
