'''3-Aminoisobutyric acid''' (or '''β-aminoisobutyric acid''', '''BAIBA''') is a product formed by the catabolism of [[thymine]].

During exercise, the increase of [[PGC-1a|PGC-1α protein]] triggers the secretion of BAIBA from exercising muscles to blood (concentration 2 to 3 µM in human serum). When BAIBA reaches the white fat tissue, it activates the expression of thermogenic genes via [[PPARA|PPARα receptors]], resulting in a browning of white fat cells.<ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1016/j.cmet.2013.12.003| pmid = 24411942| pmc = 4017355| title = Β-Aminoisobutyric Acid Induces Browning of White Fat and Hepatic β-Oxidation and is Inversely Correlated with Cardiometabolic Risk Factors| journal = Cell Metabolism| volume = 19| issue = 1| pages = 96–108| year = 2014| last1 = Roberts| first1 = Lee D.| last2 = Boström| first2 = Pontus| last3 = o'Sullivan| first3 = John F.| last4 = Schinzel| first4 = Robert T.| last5 = Lewis| first5 = Gregory D.| last6 = Dejam| first6 = Andre| last7 = Lee| first7 = Youn-Kyoung| last8 = Palma| first8 = Melinda J.| last9 = Calhoun| first9 = Sondra| last10 = Georgiadi| first10 = Anastasia| last11 = Chen| first11 = Ming-Huei| last12 = Ramachandran| first12 = Vasan S.| last13 = Larson| first13 = Martin G.| last14 = Bouchard| first14 = Claude| last15 = Rankinen| first15 = Tuomo| last16 = Souza| first16 = Amanda L.| last17 = Clish| first17 = Clary B.| last18 = Wang| first18 = Thomas J.| last19 = Estall| first19 = Jennifer L.| last20 = Soukas| first20 = Alexander A.| last21 = Cowan| first21 = Chad A.| last22 = Spiegelman| first22 = Bruce M.| last23 = Gerszten| first23 = Robert E.}}</ref> One of the consequences of the BAIBA activity is the increase of the background metabolism of the BAIBA target cells.

It has recently been postulated to play a role in cell metabolism, how body burns fat and regulates insulin, triglycerides, and total cholesterol.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://medicalxpress.com/news/2014-01-molecule-benefits.html | title = Researchers discover molecule behind the benefits of exercise | publisher = Medical Express}}</ref>{{medrs|date=February 2016}}
