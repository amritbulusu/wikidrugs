'''Dicobalt edetate''' is the [[coordination compound]] with the approximate formula Co<sub>2</sub>([[EDTA]])(H<sub>2</sub>O)<sub>6</sub>.  

Solutions of this solid have been used in Europe as an antidote to [[cyanide poisoning]].<ref name="pmid2866807">{{cite journal |author=Pickering WG |title=Cyanide toxicity and the hazards of dicobalt edetate |journal=Br Med J (Clin Res Ed) |volume=291 |issue=6509 |pages=1644 |date=December 1985 |pmid=2866807 |pmc=1418389 |doi= 10.1136/bmj.291.6509.1644-a|url=}}</ref>

It is a derivative of the (non-natural) amino acid [[ethylenediaminetetraacetate]].

==Structure==
The compound is [[coordination polymer|polymeric]] in the crystalline form.  Half of the Co<sup>2+</sup> ions are bound to the EDTA<sup>2&minus;</sup> and the other Co<sup>2+</sup> ions are bound to four water ligands as well as [[carboxylate]] [[ligand]]s on the [Co(EDTA)]<sup>2&minus;</sup> entity.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = E. F. K. Mccandlish | first1 = T. K. Michael | last2 = Rose | first2 = N. J. | year = 1978 | title = Comparison of the Structures and Aqueous Solutions of [(O-Phenylenediaminetetraacetato(2-)]Cobalt(II) and [Ethylenediaminetetraacetato(2-)]Cobalt(II) | journal = [[Inorg. Chem.]] | volume = 17 | pages = 1383–94 | doi = 10.1021/ic50184a001 | last3 = Neal | first3 = J. A. | last4 = Lingafelter | first4 = E. C. | last5 = Rose | first5 = N. J. | issue = 6}}</ref> In aqueous solution, depolymerization occurs to give [Co(EDTA)]<sup>2&minus;</sup> and [Co(H<sub>2</sub>O)<sub>6</sub>]<sup>2+</sup> ions, each of which is kinetically labile and has a high affinity for cyanide.
