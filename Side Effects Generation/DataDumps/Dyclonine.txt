'''Dyclonine''' ('''Dyclocaine''') is an oral [[Anesthesia|anaesthetic]] that is the active ingredient of '''Sucrets''', an [[Over-the-counter substance|over the counter]] throat lozenge.<ref>{{cite encyclopedia |author=Janice Jorgensen |coauthors= |editor= |encyclopedia=

==History==
The product Sucrets was introduced in [[Baltimore, Maryland]], by [[Sharp & Dohme]] in 1932.<ref name=tin/>

In 1966 the [[Federal Trade Commission]] ordered [[Merck and Company]] to discontinue the [[false claim]]s of germ-killing and pain-relieving properties for its Sucrets and Children's Sucrets throat lozenges.<ref>{{cite news |author= |coauthors= |title=F.T.C. Bids Merck Halt Claims That Lozenges Will Kill Germs |url=https://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=FA0C15F63F58107B93C2AB178FD85F428685F9 |quote= |newspaper=[[Associated Press]] in the [[New York Times]] |date=April 19, 1966 |accessdate=2011-09-24 }}</ref> In 1977, it was acquired by [[Beecham (pharmaceutical company)|Beecham]], later merging with [[SmithKline Beckman]] in 1989 to form SmithKline Beecham. By 1994 the brand switched from a metal container to a [[plastic container]].<ref name=tin>{{cite news |author= |coauthors= |title=The Sucrets tin joins the age of plastics |url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/USAToday/access/55245690.html?dids=55245690:55245690&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Jul+19%2C+1994&author=Michelle+Healy&pub=USA+TODAY+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=The+Sucrets+tin+joins+the+age+of+plastics&pqatl=google |quote=Invented in Baltimore by Sharp & Dohme pharmaceutical in 1932, Sucrets have always been sold in the trademark metal box except for one 4 1/2-month period during the late 1960s when a tin shortage led to cardboard packaging, says [Frank Dzvonik]. |newspaper=[[USA Today]] |date=July 19, 1994 |accessdate=2011-09-24 }}</ref>
SmithKline Beecham, after announcing a merger with GlaxoWellcome to form [[GlaxoSmithKline]], sold the brand in 2000 to [[Insight Pharmaceuticals]]. In 2011, Sucrets reintroduced their product back into the familiar tin due to popular demand and nostalgia.
