'''''Versed''''' is a book of poetry written by [[Rae Armantrout]] and published by [[Wesleyan University Press]] in 2009 (see [[2009 in poetry]]).  It won the [[National Book Critics Circle Award#2009|2009 National Book Critics Circle Award]] for Poetry and the [[2010 Pulitzer Prize]] [[Pulitzer Prize for Poetry#2010s|for Poetry]] after being named a finalist for the [[National Book Award for Poetry|National Book Award]].<ref>http://versedreader.site.wesleyan.edu</ref><ref>http://bookcritics.org/awards/past_awards/</ref> Armantrout is only the third poet to win two out of these three awards in one year.<ref>[http://www.newyorker.com/arts/critics/books/2010/05/17/100517crbo_books_chiasson Entangled: The poetry of Rae Armantrout] essay in ''[[The New Yorker]]'' by [[Dan Chiasson]]</ref>

==Awards==
As part of a lead-in to their awards announcement,<ref>{{cite web| title= Congratulations to NBCC award winners | work= Chapter & Verse blog  | url= http://www.csmonitor.com/Books/chapter-and-verse/2010/0312/Congratulations-to-NBCC-award-winners | author= Marjorie Kehe | date= March 12, 2010  | publisher= [[The Christian Science Monitor]] | accessdate=2010-04-13}}</ref> [[National Book Critics Circle|NBCC]] board member James Marcus called ''Versed'' a collection of "vigilant, often beautiful poems [that] seem to reset the readerâ€™s mental instrumentation&mdash;what Armantrout calls the '[[whirligig]] / of attention, / the figuring and / reconfiguring / of charges / among orbits / (obits) / that has taken forever.'"<ref>{{cite web| title= 30 Books in 30 Days: Versed, by Rae Armantrout | work=Critical Mass Blog | author=James Marcus | date= March 9, 2010  | url= http://bookcritics.org/blog/archive/30_books_in_30_days_versed_by_rae_armantrout/ | publisher= [[National Book Critics Circle]] | accessdate=2010-04-13}}</ref>

According to the [[Pulitzer Prize#Board|Pulitzer Prize Board]], ''Versed'' is a "book striking for its wit and linguistic inventiveness, offering poems that are often little thought-bombs detonating in the mind long after the first reading."<ref>{{cite web| title= The 2010 Pulitzer Prize Winners: Poetry | url= http://www.pulitzer.org/citation/2010-Poetry | publisher= The Pulitzer Prizes | accessdate=2010-04-13}}</ref>
