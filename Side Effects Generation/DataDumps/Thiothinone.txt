'''Thiothinone''' ('''βk-MPA''') is a [[stimulant]] of the [[Substituted cathinone|cathinone]] class that is the beta-keto [[structural analog|analog]] of [[methiopropamine]] and has been sold online as a [[designer drug]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.caymanchem.com/Product.vm/catalog/9002101 | title=2-Thiothinone (hydrochloride) | publisher=Cayman Chemical | accessdate=9 September 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | url=http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/ja01255a001 | title=α-Thienylaminoalkanes |author1=F. F. Blicke |author2=J. H. Burckhalter | journal=Journal of the American Chemical Society |date=March 1942  | volume=64 | issue=3 | pages=477–480 | doi=10.1021/ja01255a001}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | author1=Veniero Gambaro | url=https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11419-015-0289-2 | title=Identification and characterization of a new designer drug thiothinone in seized products | date=January 2016 | author2=Eleonora Casagni | author3=Lucia Dell’Acqua | author4=Gabriella Roda | author5=Lucia Tamborini | author6=Giacomo Luca Visconti | author7=Francesco Demartin | journal=Forensic Toxicology | volume=34 | issue=1 | pages=174–178 | doi=10.1007/s11419-015-0289-2}}</ref>

Thiothinone was also reported to be a [[pyrolysis]] product of methiopropamine.<ref>{{cite journal | url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/dta.1571/abstract | title=Characterization of the pyrolysis products of methiopropamine |author1=Emily D. Bouso |author2=Elizabeth A. Gardner |author3=John E. O'Brien |author4=Brian Talbot |author5=Pierce V. Kavanagh | journal=Drug Testing and Analysis |date=July–August 2014 | volume=6 | issue=7–8 | pages=676–683 | doi=10.1002/dta.1571 | pmid=24166818}}</ref>
