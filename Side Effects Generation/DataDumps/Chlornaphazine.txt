'''Chlornaphazine''', a derivative of [[2-Naphthylamine|2-naphthylamine]], is a [[nitrogen mustard]] that was developed in the 1950s for the treatment of [[polycythemia]] and [[Hodgkin's disease]].<ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1111/j.0954-6820.1954.tb11446.x |author1=Videbaek, A. |author2=Kaae, S. | title = 2-Naphthylbis(chloroethyl)amine in the treatment of malignant diseases, particularly Hodgkin's disease | journal = Acta Medica Scandinavica  | year = 1954 | volume = 149 | pages = 361–368 | pmid = 13180246 | issue = 5}}</ref>   However, a high incidence of [[bladder cancer]]s in patients receiving treatment with chlornaphthazine led to use of the drug being discontinued.<ref>{{cite journal | author = Benedicte Laursen | journal = Br Med J. | year = 1970 | volume = 3 | issue = 5724 | pages = 684–685 | title = Cancer of the Bladder in Patients Treated with Chlornaphazine | pmc = 1701774 | doi = 10.1136/bmj.3.5724.684 | pmid = 5470116}}</ref>

The [[International Agency for Research on Cancer]] has listed chlornaphazine as a human [[carcinogen]].<ref>[http://www.inchem.org/documents/iarc/suppl7/chlornaphazine.html N,N-Bis(2-Chloroethyl)-2-Naphthylamine (Chlornaphazine)], [[International Agency for Research on Cancer]]</ref>
