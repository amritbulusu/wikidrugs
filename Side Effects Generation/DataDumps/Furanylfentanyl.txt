'''Furanylfentanyl''' ('''Fu-F''') is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] that is an [[structural analog|analog]] of [[fentanyl]]<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Jerome R.|last1=Bagley|first2=Richard L.|last2=Wynn|first3=Frieda G.|last3=Rudo|first4=Brian M.|last4=Doorley|title=New 4-(Heteroanilido)piperidines, Structurally Related to the Pure Opioid Agonist Fentanyl, with Agonist and/or Antagonist Properties|journal=Journal of Medicinal Chemistry|date=March 1989|pages=663–671|volume=32|issue=3|doi=10.1021/jm00123a028|pmid=2563773|first5=H. Kenneth|last5=Spencer|first6=Theodore|last6=Spaulding}}</ref> and has been sold as a [[designer drug]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://nsddb.eu/substance/567/ | title=Furanylfentanyl | publisher=New Synthetic Drugs Database | work=[[European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction|EMCDDA]]}}</ref> It has an [[Effective dose (pharmacology)|ED<sub>50</sub>]] value of 0.02&nbsp;mg/kg in mice.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://patents.google.com/patent/US4584303 | title=Patent US4584303 - N-aryl-N-(4-piperidinyl)amides and pharmaceutical compositions and method employing such compounds | work=The Boc Group, Inc | date=9 April 1984 | author1=Bao-Shan Huang |author2=Ross C. Terrell |author3=Kirsten H. Deutsche |author4=Linas V. Kudzma | author5=Nhora L. Lalinde}}</ref> This makes it approximately five times less potent than fentanyl.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Quan|last1=Chen|first2=You|last2=Shang|first3=Yong|last3=Xu|first4=Ping|last4=Li|title=Analgesic effect and pharmacological mechanism of fentanyl and butorphanol in a rat model of incisional pain|journal=Journal of Clinical Anesthesia|date=February 2016|pages=67–73|volume=28|doi=10.1016/j.jclinane.2015.08.010|pmid=26440441|first5=Ping|last5=Li|first6=Guo-Li|last6=Liu}}</ref>

== Side effects ==

Side effects of [[fentanyl]] analogs are similar to those of fentanyl itself, which include [[itching]], [[nausea]] and potentially serious [[respiratory depression]], which can be life-threatening. Fentanyl analogs have killed hundreds of people throughout Europe and the former Soviet republics since the most recent resurgence in use began in Estonia in the early 2000s, and novel derivatives continue to appear.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Jane|last1=Mounteney|first2=Isabelle|last2=Giraudon|first3=Gleb|last3=Denissov|first4=Paul|last4=Griffiths|title=Fentanyls: Are we missing the signs? Highly potent and on the rise in Europe|journal=International Journal of Drug Policy|date=July 2015|pages=626–631|volume=26|issue=7|doi=10.1016/j.drugpo.2015.04.003|pmid=25976511}}</ref>

Life-threatening adverse reactions caused by furanylfentanyl use have been observed in Sweden<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Anders|last1=Helander|first2=Matilda|last2=Bäckberg|first3=Olof|last3=Beck|title=Intoxications involving the fentanyl analogs acetylfentanyl, 4-methoxybutyrfentanyl and furanylfentanyl: results from the Swedish STRIDA project|journal=Clinical Toxicology|date=20 April 2016|pages=324–332|volume=54|issue=4|pmid=26850293|doi=10.3109/15563650.2016.1139715}}</ref> and Canada.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Salman A.|last1=Klar|first2=Elizabeth|last2=Brodkin|first3=Erin|last3=Gibson|first4=Shovita|last4=Padhi|title=Furanyl-Fentanyl Overdose Events Caused by Smoking Contaminated Crack Cocaine — British Columbia, Canada, July 15–18, 2016|url=https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/65/wr/mm6537a6.htm|journal=MMWR. Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report|date=23 September 2016|pages=1015–1016|volume=65|issue=37|doi=10.15585/mmwr.mm6537a6|pmid=27657853|first5=Christine|last5=Predy|first6=Corey|last6=Green|first7=Victoria|last7=Lee}}</ref> At least seven deaths in Cook County, Illinois, have been linked to furanylfentanyl in 2016, with additional deaths in suburban Chicago in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cookcountyil.gov/2016/04/18/medical-examiner-reports-deaths-from-powerful-opioids-on-the-rise/ |title=Medical Examiner Reports Deaths from Powerful Opioids on the Rise |publisher=Cook County Government |date=18 April 2016 |author=Toni Preckwinkle |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160602234200/http://www.cookcountyil.gov/2016/04/18/medical-examiner-reports-deaths-from-powerful-opioids-on-the-rise/ |archivedate=2016-06-02 |df= }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|first1=Amanda L. == Legal status ==
Furanylfentanyl is illegal in Sweden as of January 2016.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.folkhalsomyndigheten.se/nyheter-och-press/nyhetsarkiv/2015/november/31-nya-amnen-kan-klassas-som-narkotika-eller-halsofarlig-vara/ | title=31 nya ämnen kan klassas som narkotika eller hälsofarlig vara | publisher=Folkhälsomyndigheten | language=sv | date=17 November 2015}}</ref>

The United States [[Drug Enforcement Administration]] (DEA) proposed a temporary placement of furanylfentanyl into Schedule I of the [[Controlled Substances Act]] on 27 September 2016.<ref name="DEA">{{Cite web|title=Proposed Rule: Schedules of Controlled Substances: Temporary Placement of Furanyl Fentanyl Into Schedule I|author=Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA)|publisher=Federal Register|date=27 September 2016|url=https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2016/09/27/2016-23183/schedules-of-controlled-substances-temporary-placement-of-furanyl-fentanyl-into-schedule-i}}</ref> On November 29, 2016, the DEA issued its final rule, making furanylfentanyl Schedule I.<ref>https://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/fed_regs/rules/2016/fr1129.htm</ref><ref>https://www.forbes.com/sites/davidkroll/2016/11/30/furanyl-fentanyl-joins-u-47700-as-the-second-illicit-opioid-dea-banned-in-november/#310b1bb42dba</ref>

== Chinese manufacture ==
According to the DEA,<ref>{{cite web | first1=Ashley | last1=Welch | url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/fentanyl-what-you-need-to-know-about-deadly-opioid/ | title=Fentanyl: What you need to know about the deadly opioid | date=5 April 2016 | agency=CBS News | ref=DEA}}</ref> fentanyl and its analog are being mass-produced in clandestine labs in China and are then smuggled into the United States via traditional distribution routes through Mexico. These new variants of fentanyl are manufactured by Chinese chemists in order to circumvent restrictions on the sale of fentanyl in the United States.<ref>{{cite web | first1=David | last1=Armstrong | url=https://www.statnews.com/2016/04/26/chinese-sell-modified-fentanyl/ | title=Chinese labs modify deadly fentanyl to circumvent ban on sales to US | publisher=Stat News | date=26 April 2016}}</ref>
