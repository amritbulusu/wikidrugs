'''Epsiprantel''' (trade name '''Cestex''') is a [[veterinary drug]] which is used as an [[anthelmintic]] against [[tapeworm]]s such as ''[[Echinococcus granulosus]]''.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=Arru, E. |author2=Garippa, G. |author3=Manger, B. R. | title = Efficacy of epsiprantel against Echinococcus granulosus infections in dogs | journal = Research in Veterinary Science | year = 1990 | volume = 49 | issue = 3 | pages = 378â€“379 | pmid = 2267429}}</ref>

==Indications==
It is indicated for the removal of [[Cestoda|cestodes]] (tapeworms) in cats (''[[Dipylidium caninum]]'' and ''[[Taenia taeniaeformis]]'') and dogs (''Dipylidium caninum'' and ''[[Taenia pisiformis]]'') 7 weeks of age and older.<ref name="PI" />
