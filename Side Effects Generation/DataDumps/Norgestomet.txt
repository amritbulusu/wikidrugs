'''Norgestomet''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]]) (brand name '''Syncro-Mate B'''; former developmental code name '''SC-21009'''),[http://krex.k-state.edu/dspace/handle/2097/27088] or '''norgestamet''', also known as '''11β-methyl-17α-acetoxy-19-norprogesterone''', is a [[steroid]]al [[progestin]] of the [[19-norprogesterone]] group used in [[veterinary medicine]] to control [[estrus]] and [[ovulation]] in [[cattle]].<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA887|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=887–}}</ref><ref name="GebeleinDunn1990">{{cite book|author1=Charles G. Gebelein|author2=Richard L. Dunn|title=Progress in Biomedical Polymers|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-qXTBH9-an0C&pg=PA356|date=1 July 1990|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-0-306-43523-2|pages=356–}}</ref> It was developed and introduced in the mid-1970s.<ref name="Gordon2013">{{cite book|author=I. Gordon|title=Controlled Breeding in Farm Animals|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=mEgvBQAAQBAJ&pg=PA40|date=22 October 2013|publisher=Elsevier|isbn=978-1-4832-8569-6|pages=40–}}</ref> In addition to the [[progesterone receptor]], norgestomet possesses weak ([[micromolar]]) [[affinity (pharmacology)|affinity]] for the [[glucocorticoid receptor]].<ref name="pmid8467644">{{cite journal | vauthors = Moffatt RJ, Zollers WG, Welshons WV, Kieborz KR, Garverick HA, Smith MF | title = Basis of norgestomet action as a progestogen in cattle | journal = Domest. Anim. Endocrinol. | volume = 10 | issue = 1 | pages = 21–30 | year = 1993 | pmid = 8467644 | doi = | url = }}</ref>
