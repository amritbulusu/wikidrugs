'''Econazole''' (commonly used as the [[nitrate]] salt) is an [[antifungal medication]] of the [[imidazole]] class.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Thienpont | first1 = D | last2 = Van Cutsem | first2 = J | last3 = Van Nueten | first3 = JM | last4 = Niemegeers | first4 = CJ | last5 = Marsboom | first5 = R | title = Bilogical and toxicological properties of econazole, a broad-spectrum antimycotic | journal = Arzneimittel-Forschung | volume = 25 | issue = 2 | pages = 224–30 | year = 1975 | pmid = 1173036}}</ref> It is sold under the brand names Spectrazole ([[United States]]) and Ecostatin ([[Canada]]), among others. It is a component of Pevisone and Ecoderm-TA<ref>[http://www.rephco-bd.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=22&Itemid=48 Product descriptions] at REPHCO Pharmaceuticals Limited, Bangladesh. Retrieved June 2012</ref> ([[econazole/triamcinolone]]).

==Medical uses==
Econazole is used as a [[Cream (pharmaceutical)|cream]] to treat [[skin infection]]s such as [[athlete's foot]], [[tinea]], [[pityriasis versicolor]], [[ringworm]], and [[jock itch]]. It is also sold in Canada under the brand name Ecostatin as vaginal ovules to treat [[vagina]]l [[candidiasis|thrush]].

Econazole nitrate exhibits strong anti-feeding properties against the keratin-digesting common clothes moth ''[[Tineola bisselliella]]''.<ref>Sunderland, M. R.; Cruickshank, R. H.; Leighs, S. J. (2014). “The efficacy of antifungal azole and antiprotozoal compounds in protection of wool from keratin-digesting insect larvae”. ''Textile Research Journal'' '''84''' (9): 924–931. http://trj.sagepub.com/content/84/9/924</ref> 

==Adverse effects==
About  3% of patients treated with econazole nitrate cream reported side effects.  The most common symptoms were burning, itching, [[erythema]], and one outbreak of a [[pruritic|pruritic rash]].<ref>Perrigo Econazole Nitrate Cream Product Description. 2012.</ref>

==Synthesis==
Imidazoles devoid of the [[nitro group]] no longer have any [[antiprotozoal]] activity, however, such drugs are effective [[antifungal agents]].
[[File:Econazole synthesis.svg|thumb|center|555px|Econazole synthesis:<ref>{{Cite journal | doi = 10.1021/jm00305a014| title = Preparation and antimycotic properties of derivatives of 1-phenethylimidazole| journal = Journal of Medicinal Chemistry| volume = 12| issue = 5| pages = 784| year = 1969| last1 = Godefroi | first1 = E. F. | last2 = Heeres | first2 = J. | last3 = Van Cutsem | first3 = J. | last4 = Janssen | first4 = P. A. J. }}</ref> {{Cite patent|DE|1940388}} {{US patent|3717655}} (1970, 1973 both to [[Janssen Pharmaceutica|Janssen]]).]]
Alkylation of [[imidazole]] ('''2''') with bromoketone ('''1''') prepared from ''o,p''-dichloro[[acetophenone]] affords the displacement product ('''3'''). Reduction of the ketone with [[sodium borohydride]] gives the corresponding alcohol ('''4'''). Alkylation of the [[alkoxide]] from that alcohol with ''p''-chlorobenzyl chloride leads to econazole ('''5'''); alkylation with ''o,p''-dichlorobenzyl chloride gives [[miconazole]].
