'''Fenestrel''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]) (developmental code name '''ORF-3858''') is a [[Organic compound#Synthetic compounds|synthetic]], [[nonsteroidal]] [[estrogen]] that was developed as a [[emergency contraception|postcoital contraceptive]] in the 1960s but was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA517|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=517–}}</ref><ref name="pmid5544232">{{cite journal | vauthors = Revaz C, Goldenberg B, Achtari H | title = [Critical study of new contraceptive methods] | language = French | journal = Schweiz Med Wochenschr | volume = 101 | issue = 3 | pages = 92–6 | year = 1971 | pmid = 5544232 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid21698041">{{cite journal | vauthors = Kunjappu MJ | title = Pioneering studies of the "morning-after" pill | journal = Yale J Biol Med | volume = 84 | issue = 2 | pages = 109–11 | year = 2011 | pmid = 21698041 | pmc = 3117403 | doi = | url = }}</ref> Synthesized by [[Ortho Pharmaceutical]] in 1961 and studied extensively, it was coined the "morning-after-pill" or "postcoital antifertility agent".<ref name="GlasserBullock2012">{{cite book|author1=Stanley R. Glasser|author2=David W. Bullock|title=Cellular and Molecular Aspects of Implantation|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iknTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA478|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-1-4613-3180-3|pages=478–}}</ref> Fenestrel is a seco [[structural analog|analogue]] of [[doisynolic acid]], and a member of the [[cyclohexenecarboxylic acid]] series of estrogens.<ref name="KirkOthmer1980">{{cite book|author1=Raymond Eller Kirk|author2=Donald Frederick Othmer|title=Encyclopedia of chemical technology|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yb9TAAAAMAAJ|year=1980|publisher=Wiley|isbn=978-0-471-02065-3|page=672}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Acta europaea fertilitatis|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KP-qAAAAIAAJ|year=1969|publisher=Piccin Medical Books.}}</ref>
