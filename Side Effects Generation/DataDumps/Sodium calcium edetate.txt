<!-- Definition and medical uses -->
'''Sodium calcium edetate''' ('''sodium calcium EDTA'''), also known as '''edetate calcium disodium''' among other names, is a medication primarily used to treat [[lead poisoning]].<ref name=WHO2008/> This includes short term and long term lead poisoning.<ref name=AHFS2017/> For [[lead encephalopathy]] it is typically used together with [[dimercaprol]].<ref name=AHFS2017/> It does not appear to be useful for [[tetraethyllead]] toxicity.<ref name=AHFS2017/> It is given by [[intravenous infusion|slow injection into a vein]] or [[intramuscular injection|into a muscle]].<ref name=WHO2008>{{cite book|title=WHO Model Formulary 2008|date=2009|publisher=World Health Organization|isbn=9789241547659|page=X|url=http://apps.who.int/medicinedocs/documents/s16879e/s16879e.pdf|accessdate=8 January 2017|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161213060118/http://apps.who.int/medicinedocs/documents/s16879e/s16879e.pdf|archivedate=13 December 2016|df=}}</ref>

<!-- Side effects and mechanism -->
Common side effects include pain at the site of injection.<ref name=AHFS2017>{{cite web|title=Edetate Calcium Disodium|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/edetate-calcium-disodium.html|publisher=The American Society of Health-System Pharmacists|accessdate=8 January 2017|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116204307/https://www.drugs.com/monograph/edetate-calcium-disodium.html|archivedate=16 January 2017|df=}}</ref> Other side effects may include [[kidney problems]], diarrhea, fever, muscle pains, and [[low blood pressure]].<ref name=WHO2008/> Benefits when needed in [[pregnancy]] are likely greater than the risks.<ref name=AHFS2017/> Sodium calcium edetate is in the [[chelating agent]] family of medication.<ref name=AHFS2017/> It is a salt of [[edetate]] with two [[sodium]] and one [[calcium]] atoms.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Kasture|first1=Dr A. V.|title=Pharmaceutical Chemistry - I|date=2008|publisher=Pragati Books Pvt. Ltd.|isbn=9788185790121|page=16.11|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=ZkoJsQIhDWkC&pg=SA16-PA11|language=en|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116152923/https://books.google.ca/books?id=ZkoJsQIhDWkC&pg=SA16-PA11|archivedate=2017-01-16|df=}}</ref> It works by binding a number of heavy metals which allows them to leave the body in the urine.<ref name=AHFS2017/>

<!-- History and culture -->
Sodium calcium edetate came into medical use in the United States in 1953.<ref name=AHFS2017/> It is on the [[World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines]], the most effective and safe medicines needed in a [[health system]].<ref name=WHO19th>{{cite web|title=WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (19th List)|url=http://www.who.int/medicines/publications/essentialmedicines/EML_2015_FINAL_amended_NOV2015.pdf?ua=1|work=World Health Organization|accessdate=8 December 2016|date=April 2015|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161213052708/http://www.who.int/medicines/publications/essentialmedicines/EML_2015_FINAL_amended_NOV2015.pdf?ua=1|archivedate=13 December 2016|df=}}</ref> In the United States a course of treatment costs 50 to 100 USD.<ref name=Ric2015>{{cite book|last1=Hamilton|first1=Richart|title=Tarascon Pocket Pharmacopoeia 2015 Deluxe Lab-Coat Edition|date=2015|publisher=Jones & Bartlett Learning|isbn=9781284057560|page=471}}</ref> [[Edetate disodium]] is a different formulation which does not have the same effects.<ref name=AHFS2017/>

==Medical uses==
The primarily use is to treat [[lead poisoning]].<ref name=WHO2008/> In lead toxicity it is an alternative to [[succimer]].<ref name=AHFS2017/>

It may also be used for [[plutonium]] toxicity.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Flanagan|first1=Robert|last2=Jones|first2=Alison|last3=Maynard|first3=Robert L.|title=Antidotes: Principles and Clinical Applications|date=2003|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=9780203485071|page=47|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=Ux0-AAAAIAAJ&pg=PA47|language=en|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116151507/https://books.google.ca/books?id=Ux0-AAAAIAAJ&pg=PA47|archivedate=2017-01-16|df=}}</ref>
