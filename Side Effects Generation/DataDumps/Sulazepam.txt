'''Sulazepam''' is a [[benzodiazepine]] derivative. It is the [[thioamide]] derivative of [[diazepam]]. It is metabolised into [[diazepam]], [[desmethyldiazepam]] and [[temazepam|oxydiazepam]]. It has [[sedative]], [[muscle relaxant]], [[hypnotic]], [[anticonvulsant]] and [[anxiolytic]] properties like those of other benzodiazepines.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.psychotropics.dk/moleculeView/default.aspx?ID=1444&Catalogtype=A&ChapterID=1&Thissortorder=41 | title = sulazepam | accessdate = 29 December 2008 | year = 2003 | publisher = psychotropics.dk }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author=Golovenko NIa, Zin'kovskii VG |title=(title in Russian) |trans_title=Analysis of the structure of the components of the convulsive action of corazole following administration of sulazepam and its metabolites to mice |language=Russian |journal=Biull Eksp Biol Med |volume=82 |issue=9 |pages=1078â€“1081 |date=September 1976 |pmid=11012 |doi= |url=}}</ref> It was never marketed.
