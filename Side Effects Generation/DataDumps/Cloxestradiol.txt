'''Cloxestradiol''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known as '''17-(2,2,2-trichloroethoxy)estradiol''', is a [[Organic compound#Synthetic compounds|synthetic]], [[steroid]]al [[estrogen]] that was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA308|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=308â€“}}</ref> It is an [[structural analog|analogue]] of [[estradiol]] with a 2,2,2-trichloroethoxy substitution.<ref name="Elks2014" /> The ''O'',''O''-diacetate derivative, [[cloxestradiol acetate]] (brand name Genovul), has been marketed as an estrogen.<ref name="Elks2014" />
