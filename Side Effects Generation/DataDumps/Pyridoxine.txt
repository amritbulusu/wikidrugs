<!-- Definition and medical uses -->
'''Pyridoxine''', also known as '''vitamin B<sub>6</sub>''' and '''pyridoxol''', is a form of [[vitamin B6|vitamin B<sub>6</sub>]] found commonly in food and used as [[dietary supplement]].<ref name=WHO2008>{{cite book|title=WHO Model Formulary 2008|date=2009|publisher=World Health Organization|isbn=9789241547659|page=496|url=http://apps.who.int/medicinedocs/documents/s16879e/s16879e.pdf|accessdate= 8 December 2016}}</ref> As a supplement it is used to treat and prevent [[pyridoxine deficiency]], [[sideroblastic anaemia]], [[pyridoxine-dependent epilepsy]], certain [[metabolic disorders]], problems from [[isoniazid]], and certain types of [[mushroom poisoning]].<ref name=AHFS2016/><ref name=WHO2008/> It is used by mouth or by injection.<ref name=AHFS2016/>

<!-- Side effects and mechanisms -->
It is usually well tolerated.<ref name=AHFS2016/> Occasionally side effects include headache, numbness, and sleepiness.<ref name=AHFS2016/> Normal doses are safe during [[pregnancy]] and [[breastfeeding]].<ref name=AHFS2016/> Pyridoxine is in the [[vitamin B]] family of vitamins.<ref name=AHFS2016/> It is required by the body to make [[amino acids]], [[carbohydrates]], and [[lipids]].<ref name=AHFS2016>{{cite web|title=Pyridoxine Hydrochloride|url=https://www.drugs.com/monograph/pyridoxine-hydrochloride.html|publisher=The American Society of Health-System Pharmacists|accessdate= 8 December 2016}}</ref> Sources in the diet include [[fruit]], [[vegetables]], and [[food grain|grain]].<ref name=NIH2016>{{cite web|title=Office of Dietary Supplements - Dietary Supplement Fact Sheet: Vitamin B6|url=https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/VitaminB6-HealthProfessional/|website=ods.od.nih.gov|accessdate=30 December 2016|date=11 February 2016}}</ref>

<!-- History and culture -->
Pyridoxine was discovered in 1934, isolated in 1938, and first made in 1939.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Squires|first1=Victor R.|title=The Role of Food, Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries in Human Nutrition - Volume IV|date=2011|publisher=EOLSS Publications|isbn=9781848261952|page=121|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=VJWoCwAAQBAJ&pg=PA121|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Harris|first1=Harry|title=Advances in Human Genetics 6|date=2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=9781461582649|page=39|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=UJ0KCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA39|language=en}}</ref> It is on the [[World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines]], the most effective and safe medicines needed in a [[health system]].<ref name=WHO19th>{{cite web|title=WHO Model List of Essential Medicines (19th List)|url=http://www.who.int/medicines/publications/essentialmedicines/EML_2015_FINAL_amended_NOV2015.pdf?ua=1|work=World Health Organization|accessdate=8 December 2016|date=April 2015}}</ref> Pyridoxine is available as a [[generic medication]] and [[over the counter]].<ref name=AHFS2016/> The wholesale cost in the [[developing world]] is about 0.59 to 3.54 USD per month.<ref name=ERC2014>{{cite web|title=Vitamin B6|url=http://erc.msh.org/dmpguide/resultsdetail.cfm?language=english&code=PDX50T&s_year=2014&year=2014&str=50%20mg&desc=Vitamin%20B6&pack=new&frm=TAB-CAP&rte=PO&class_code2=27%2E&supplement=&class_name=%2827%2E%29Vitamins%20and%20minerals%3Cbr%3E|website=International Drug Price Indicator Guide|accessdate=8 December 2016}}</ref> Foods, such as [[breakfast cereal]] have pyridoxine added in some countries.<ref name=NIH2016/>
