'''Bisdisulizole disodium''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]<ref>http://ec.europa.eu/enterprise/cosmetics/cosing/index.cfm?fuseaction=search.detailsPDF&id=75792{{dead link|date=July 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>/[[United States Adopted Name|USAN]],<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ama-assn.org/ama1/pub/upload/mm/365/bisdiulizole_disodiu.pdf | title = Statement on a nonproprietary name adopted by the USAN Council: Bisdisulizole disodium | date = April 4, 2006 | accessdate = 2008-01-08 | publisher = [[American Medical Association]]}}</ref> [[trade name]] '''Neo Heliopan AP''', [[INCI]] disodium phenyl dibenzimidazole tetrasulfonate) is a water-soluble [[organic compound]] which is added to [[sunscreen]]s to absorb [[Ultraviolet|UVA]] rays. It is marketed by [[Symrise]].

Bisdisulizole disodium is not approved in the United States by the [[Food and Drug Administration|FDA]], but it has been approved in the European Union since the year 2000<ref>{{cite journal | title = Über den Lichtschutzfaktor hinaus - neue effiziente und photostabile UVA-Filter | vauthors = Osstervalder U, Luther H, Herzog B  | journal = Bundesgesundheitsblatt Gesundheitsforschung Gesundheitsschutz | language = German | year = 2001 | volume = 44 | issue = 5 | pages = 463–70 | doi = 10.1007/s001030170019}}</ref> and other parts of the world.<ref>{{cite book | chapter = 10. Sunscreens | title = Australian regulatory guidelines for OTC medicines | date = July 1, 2003 | pages = 14 | chapterurl = http://www.tga.gov.au/docs/pdf/argom_10.pdf | accessdate = 2008-01-08 | publisher = [[Department of Health and Ageing (Australia)|Commonwealth Department of Health and Ageing]], [[Therapeutic Goods Administration]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.symrise.com/fileadmin/user_upload/pdf/Symrise_UV_protection_products.pdf | title = Symrise UV protection products | accessdate = 2008-08-10 | publisher = [[Symrise]]}}</ref>
