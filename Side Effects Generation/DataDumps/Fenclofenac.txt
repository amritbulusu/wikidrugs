'''Fenclofenac'''  is a [[nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug]] (NSAID) previously used in [[rheumatism]]. It has mild [[immunosuppressant|immunosuppressive]] effects<ref name="pmid7902830">{{cite journal |vauthors=Spiers EM, Watson NT, Beck JS, Chapman IV, Dettmar PW |title=The effect of fenclofenac on the regeneration of lymphocytes in rats following total body irradiation |journal=Int. J. Immunopharmacol. |volume=15 |issue=8 |pages=865–9 |date=November 1993 |pmid=7902830 |doi= 10.1016/0192-0561(93)90003-h|url=}}</ref> and may displace [[thyroid hormone]] from its [[binding protein]].<ref name="pmid7448557">{{cite journal |vauthors=Allen PA, Taylor RT |title=Fenclofenac and thyroid function tests |journal=Br Med J |volume=281 |issue=6255 |pages=1642 |date=December 1980 |pmid=7448557 |pmc=1715072 |doi= 10.1136/bmj.281.6255.1642}}</ref><ref name="pmid7427506">{{cite journal |vauthors=Taylor R, Clark F, Griffiths ID, Weeke J |title=Prospective study of effect of fenclofenac on thyroid function tests |journal=Br Med J |volume=281 |issue=6245 |pages=911–2 |date=October 1980 |pmid=7427506 |pmc=1714224 |doi= 10.1136/bmj.281.6245.911}}</ref> It can also cause [[lichen planus]].<ref name="pmid6232357">{{cite journal |vauthors=Ferguson MM, Wiesenfeld D, MacDonald DG |title=Oral mucosal lichenoid eruption due to fenclofenac |journal=J Oral Med |volume=39 |issue=1 |pages=39–40 |year=1984 |pmid=6232357 |doi= |url=}}</ref>

Due to its side effects it was withdrawn from the UK and US in the 1980s.
