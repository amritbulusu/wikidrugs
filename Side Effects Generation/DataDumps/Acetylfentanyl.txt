'''Acetylfentanyl''' ('''acetyl fentanyl''') is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] drug that is an [[analog (chemistry)|analog]] of [[fentanyl]].<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Anders|last1=Helander|first2=Matilda|last2=Bäckberg|first3=Olof|last3=Beck|title=Intoxications involving the fentanyl analogs acetylfentanyl, 4-methoxybutyrfentanyl and furanylfentanyl: results from the Swedish STRIDA project|url=http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.3109/15563650.2016.1139715|journal=Clinical Toxicology|date=20 April 2016|issn=1556-3650|pages=324–332|volume=54|issue=4|pmid=26850293|doi=10.3109/15563650.2016.1139715}}</ref> Studies have estimated acetylfentanyl to be fifteen times more potent than [[morphine]].<ref name="Higashikawa">{{cite journal|first1=Yoshiyasu|last1=Higashikawa|first2=Shinichi|last2=Suzuki|title=Studies on 1-(2-phenethyl)-4-(''N''-propionylanilino)piperidine (fentanyl) and its related compounds. VI. Structure-analgesic activity relationship for fentanyl, methyl-substituted fentanyls and other analogues|url=https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11419-007-0039-1|journal=Forensic Toxicology|date=June 2008|issn=1860-8965|pages=1–5|volume=26|issue=1|doi=10.1007/s11419-007-0039-1}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|first1=M. D.|last1=Aceto|first2=E. R.|last2=Bowman|first3=L. S.|last3=Harris|first4=E. L.|last4=May|title=Dependence studies on new compounds in the rhesus monkey, rat and mouse|url=https://archives.drugabuse.gov/pdf/monographs/81.pdf|journal=NIDA research monograph|date=1989|issn=1046-9516|pages=578–631|volume=95|pmid=2641054}}</ref> It has never been licensed for medical use and instead only been sold as a [[designer drug]]. Acetylfentanyl was discovered at the same time as fentanyl itself and had only rarely been encountered on the illicit market in the late 1980s. However, in 2013, Canadian police seized 3 kilograms of acetylfentanyl.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/montreal/extremely-potent-painkiller-hits-montreal-black-market-1.1340808 | title=Extremely potent painkiller hits Montreal black market | publisher=CBS News | date=13 May 2013}}</ref> As a [[μ-opioid receptor]] agonist, acetylfentanyl may serve as a direct substitute for heroin or other opioids. Side effects of [[fentanyl]] analogs are similar to those of fentanyl itself, which include [[itching]], [[nausea]] and potentially serious [[respiratory depression]], which can be life-threatening. Fentanyl analogs have killed hundreds of people throughout Europe and the former Soviet republics since the most recent resurgence in use began in Estonia in the early 2000s, and novel derivatives continue to appear.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Jane|last1=Mounteney|first2=Isabelle|last2=Giraudon|first3=Gleb|last3=Denissov|first4=Paul|last4=Griffiths|title=Fentanyls: Are we missing the signs? Highly potent and on the rise in Europe|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0955395915000973|journal=International Journal of Drug Policy|date=July 2015|pages=626–631|volume=26|issue=7|doi=10.1016/j.drugpo.2015.04.003|pmid=25976511}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|first1=Werawan|last1=Ruangyuttikarn|first2=Michael Y.|last2=Law|first3=Douglas E.|last3=Rollins|first4=David E.|last4=Moody|title=Detection of Fentanyl and its Analogs by Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay|url=http://jat.oxfordjournals.org/content/14/3/160|journal=Journal of Analytical Toxicology|date=May–June 1990|issn=0146-4760|pages=160–164|volume=14|issue=3|pmid=2374405|doi=10.1093/jat/14.3.160}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first1=David|last1=Kroll|title=CDC Issues Alert On Deadly New Designer Drug, Acetyl Fentanyl|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/davidkroll/2013/08/29/cdc-issues-alert-on-deadly-new-designer-drug-acetyl-fentanyl/|date=29 August 2013}}</ref>

==Deaths==

===Europe===
Acetylfentanyl has been analytically confirmed in 32 fatalities in four European member states between 2013 and August 2015, Germany (2), Poland (1), Sweden (27) and the United Kingdom (2).<ref name="EMCDDA">{{cite web | DOI=10.2810/890694 | url=http://www.emcdda.europa.eu/publications/joint-reports/acetylfentanyl | title=EMCDDA–Europol Joint Report on acetylfentanyl | publisher=[[European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction]] (EMCDDA) | date=June 2016}}</ref>

===Russia===
Twelve deaths have been associated with acetylfentanyl in Russia since 2012.<ref name="EMCDDA"/><ref>{{cite journal|first1=A.B.|last1=Melent’ev|first2=S.S.|last2=Kataev|first3=O.N.|last3=Dvorskaya|title=Identification and analytical properties of acetyl fentanyl metabolites|url=https://link.springer.com/article/10.1134%2FS1061934815020124|journal=Journal of Analytical Chemistry|date=February 2015|issn=1061-9348|pages=240–248|volume=70|issue=2|doi=10.1134/S1061934815020124}}</ref>

===USA===
The [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention]] (CDC) issued a health alert to report that between March 2013 and May 2013, 14 overdose deaths related to injected acetylfentanyl had occurred among intravenous drug users (ages between 19 and 57 years) in Rhode Island. After confirming five overdoses in one county, including a fatality, Pennsylvania asked coroners and medical examiners across the state to screen for acetylfentanyl. As a result of this investigation, Pennsylvania confirmed at least one acetylfentanyl overdose death and attributed at least 50 fatalities to either fentanyl or acetylfentanyl during the first half of 2013.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)|title=Acetyl fentanyl overdose fatalities--Rhode Island, March-May 2013|url=https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm6234a5.htm|journal=MMWR. Morbidity and mortality weekly report|date=30 August 2013|issn=1545-861X|pages=703–704|volume=62|issue=34|pmid=23985500}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|first1=MatthewJ.|last1=Lozier|first2=Molly|last2=Boyd|first3=Christina|last3=Stanley|first4=Laurie|last4=Ogilvie|title=Acetyl Fentanyl, a Novel Fentanyl Analog, Causes 14 Overdose Deaths in Rhode Island, March–May 2013|journal=Journal of Medical Toxicology|date=June 2015|issn=1556-9039|pages=208–217|volume=11|issue=2|pmid=25934111|pmc=4469714|doi=10.1007/s13181-015-0477-9|first5=Ewa|last5=King|first6=Colleen|last6=Martin|first7=Lauren|last7=Lewis}}</ref> In July 2015, the DEA informed about 52 confirmed fatalities involving acetylfentanyl in the United States between 2013 and 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/drug_chem_info/acetylfentanyl.pdf | title=Acetyl fentanyl | publisher=Drug Enforcement Administration Office of Diversion Control | date=July 2015 | author=Drug Enforcement Administration}}</ref>

===Japan===

One fatal poisoning caused by intravenous injection of a "bath salt" product containing acetylfentanyl and [[4'-Methoxy-α-pyrrolidinoheptiophenone]] has been reported in 2016.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Kosei|last1=Yonemitsu|first2=Ako|last2=Sasao|first3=Satoko|last3=Mishima|first4=Yuki|last4=Ohtsu|title=A fatal poisoning case by intravenous injection of "bath salts" containing acetyl fentanyl and 4-methoxy PV8|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0379073816303553|journal=Forensic Science International|doi=10.1016/j.forsciint.2016.08.025|first5=Yoko|last5=Nishitani|date=2016|volume=267|pages=e6–e9|pmid=27591912}}</ref>

==Legal status==

===Canada===
As an analog of fentanyl, acetylfentanyl is a [[Controlled Drugs and Substances Act#Schedule I|Schedule I]] controlled drug.<ref name="isomerdesign.com"/>

===China===

As of October, 2015 acetylfentanyl is a controlled substance in China.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sfda.gov.cn/WS01/CL0056/130753.html | title=关于印发《非药用类麻醉药品和精神药品列管办法》的通知 | publisher=China Food and Drug Administration | date=27 September 2015 | language=cn| accessdate=1 October 2015}}</ref>

===United States===
Acetylfentanyl is a [[Controlled Substances Act|Schedule I]] controlled substance as of May 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/granule/FR-2015-05-21/2015-12331 | title=80 FR 29227 - Schedules of Controlled Substances: Temporary Placement of Acetyl Fentanyl into Schedule I | publisher=U.S. Government Publishing Office (GPO) | date=21 May 2015 | accessdate=30 June 2015}}</ref>

===Switzerland===
Acetylfentanyl is illegal in Switzerland as of December 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.admin.ch/opc/de/classified-compilation/20101220/index.html | title=Verordnung des EDI über die Verzeichnisse der Betäubungsmittel, psychotropen Stoffe, Vorläuferstoffe und Hilfschemikalien | publisher=Der Bundesrat | date=1 December 2015 | language=de}}</ref>

===United Kingdom===
Acetylfentanyl was made a class A drug as an analogue of fentanyl in 1986.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1986/2230/introduction/made | title=The Misuse of Drugs Act 1971 (Modification) Order 1986 | publisher=UK Home Office | date=16 December 1986}}</ref>

==Overdose==
Acetylfentanyl overdosage has been reported to closely resemble heroin overdosage clinically. Additionally, while [[naloxone]] (Narcan) is effective in treating acetylfentanyl overdose, larger than normal doses of the antidote may be required.<ref name=Higashikawa />

===Detection in body fluids===
Acetylfentanyl may be quantitated in blood, plasma or urine by [[liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry]] to confirm a diagnosis of poisoning in hospitalized patients or to provide evidence in a medicolegal death investigation. Postmortem peripheral blood acetylfentanyl concentrations have been in a range of 89–945 μg/L in victims of acute overdosage.<ref>{{cite journal|first1=Amy L.|last1=Patton|first2=Kathryn A.|last2=Seely|first3=Sharon|last3=Pulla|first4=Nancy J.|last4=Rusch|title=Quantitative Measurement of Acetyl Fentanyl and Acetyl Norfentanyl in Human Urine by LC-MS/MS|url=http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/ac4036197|journal=Analytical Chemistry|date=February 2014|pages=1760–1766|volume=86|issue=3|doi=10.1021/ac4036197|pmid=24354295|first5=Cindy L.|last5=Moran|first6=William E.|last6=Fantegrossi|first7=Laura D.|last7=Knight|first8=Jeanna M.|last8=Marraffa|first9=Paul D.|last9=Kennedy|first10=Laura P.|last10=James|first11=Gregory W.|last11=Endres|first12=Jeffery H.|last12=Moran}}</ref><ref>{{cite book | url=http://www.biomedicalpublications.com/acetylfentanyl.pdf | title=Disposition of toxic drugs and chemicals in man | publisher=Biomedical Publications | author=Randall C.
