'''Proheptazine''' is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] from the phenazepine family. It was invented in the 1960s.<ref>Diamond J, Bruce WF, Tyson FT. Synthesis and Properties of the Analgesic DL-Î±-1,3-dimethyl-4-phenyl-4-propionoxyazacycloheptane (Proheptazine). ''Journal of Medicinal Chemistry''. 1964 Jan;7:57-60.</ref>  

Proheptazine produces similar effects to other opioids, including [[analgesia]], [[sedation]], [[euphoria]], [[dizziness]] and [[nausea]]. 

In the United States it is a Schedule I Narcotic controlled substance with an ACSCN of 9643 and a 2013 annual aggregrate manufacturing quota of zero. The salts in use are the citrate (free base conversion ratio 0.589), hydrobromide (0.773), and hydrochloride (0.883). <ref>http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/fed_regs/quotas/2014/fr0825.htm</ref><ref>http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/quotas/conv_factor/index.html</ref>
