'''Ciclindole''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]; '''WIN-27,147-2'''), also known as '''cyclindole''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]), is an [[antipsychotic]] with a [[tricyclic]] [[chemical structure|structure]] that was never marketed.<ref name="isbn0-412-46630-9">{{cite book | author = David J. Triggle | title = Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents | publisher = Chapman & Hall/CRC | location = Boca Raton | year = 1996 | pages = | isbn = 0-412-46630-9 | oclc = | doi = | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=DeX7jgInYFMC&lpg=PA540&dq=cyclindole&as_brr=3&pg=PA540#v=onepage&q&f=false}}</ref><ref name="pmid6152347">{{cite journal | author = Wood PL, McQuade PS | title = Ciclindole and flucindole: novel tetrahydrocarbazolamine neuroleptics | journal = Progress in Neuro-psychopharmacology & Biological Psychiatry | volume = 8 | issue = 4-6 | pages = 773–7 | year = 1984 | pmid = 6152347 | doi = 10.1016/0278-5846(84)90057-5| url = }}</ref> It displaces [[spiperone]] [[binding (molecular)|binding]] ''[[in vitro]]'' and elevates [[dopamine]] levels in the [[striatum]], indicating it acts as a [[D2 receptor|D<sub>2</sub> receptor]] [[receptor antagonist|antagonist]].<ref name="pmid6152347">{{cite journal | author = Wood PL, McQuade PS | title = Ciclindole and flucindole: novel tetrahydrocarbazolamine neuroleptics | journal = Progress in Neuro-psychopharmacology & Biological Psychiatry | volume = 8 | issue = 4-6 | pages = 773–7 | year = 1984 | pmid = 6152347 | doi = 10.1016/0278-5846(84)90057-5| url = }}</ref>
