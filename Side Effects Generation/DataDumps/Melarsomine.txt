'''Melarsomine''' (melaminylthioarsenate) is a [[trypanocidal agent]]. In the U.S., it is marketed under the trade names ''Immiticide'' ([[Merial]]) and ''Diroban'' ([[Zoetis]]), and is approved by the [[Food and Drug Administration|FDA's]] [[Center for Veterinary Medicine]] for the treatment of adult [[Dirofilaria immitis|heartworm]] (''Dirofilaria immitis'') infection in [[Dog|dogs]]. It is not approved for treatment in cats, or dogs in late-stage infection.<ref name="Tilley2008">{{cite book|last1=Tilley|first1=Lawrence P.|title=Manual of Canine and Feline Cardiology|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nggxUPtJsAYC&pg=PA196|year=2008|publisher=Elsevier Health Sciences|isbn=1-4160-2398-4|pages=194â€“197}}</ref><ref name="BonaguraTwedt2013">{{cite book|last1=Bonagura|first1=John D. |last2=Twedt|first2=David C.|title=Kirk's Current Veterinary Therapy XV|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KlJKAgAAQBAJ&pg=PT2502|year=2013|publisher=Elsevier Health Sciences|isbn=978-0-323-22762-9|page=2502}}</ref>
