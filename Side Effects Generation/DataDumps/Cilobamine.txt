'''Cilobamine''' is a drug which acts as a [[norepinephrine-dopamine reuptake inhibitor]] (NDRI) and has [[stimulant]] and [[antidepressant]] effects.<ref>Leeson GA, Shaath ZA, Biedenbach SA, Yarrington JT, Okerholm RA. Dose related induction of the drug metabolizing enzymes of rat liver by cilobamine. ''Fundamental and Applied Toxicology''. 1984 Apr;4(2 Pt 1):261-9. {{PMID|6724198}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal | first6 = J.| last6 = Markowitz | first5 = W.| last5 = Harrison| last7 = Tricamo | first7 = E.| pages = 201| volume = 3| journal = Human Psychopharmacology: Clinical and Experimental| doi = 10.1002/hup.470030308 | first4 = P.| last1 = Wager| year = 1988| title = Cilobamine in the treatment of atypical depression | first1 = S.| last2 = Quitkin | first3 = J.| last4 = McGrath| last3 = Stewart | first2 = F.| issue = 3 }}</ref>

It can clearly be seen that the structure is based on [[dichloroisoprenaline]] that has been fused onto the bicycloalkane scaffold.
