'''Jenkem''' is a supposed [[inhalant]] and [[hallucinogen]] created from fermented [[human waste]].<ref name=Fuller2005>Fuller, Alexandra (2005). [http://ngm.nationalgeographic.com/ngm/0509/feature6/text4.html We just want enough.] ''[[National Geographic Society|National Geographic]]'', Volume 208, Issues 1-3, p. 119</ref> In the mid-1990s, it was reported to be a popular [[street drug]] among [[Zambia]]n street children. They would put the feces and urine in a jar and cover it with a balloon then let it ferment out in the sun, then afterwards they would inhale the fumes created.<ref name=Donaldson>Donaldson, Aidan (2010). ''Encountering God in the Margins: Reflections of a Justice Volunteer.'' Veritas, {{ISBN|978-1-84730-220-5}}</ref><ref name=BBC>{{cite news |first=Ishbel |last=Matheson |title=Children high on sewage |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/africa/406067.stm |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=July 30, 1999 |accessdate=2007-07-06 }}</ref><ref name=IPS/><ref name=ap1999>[http://www.aegis.com/news/ap/1999/ap990703.html "African Children Orphaned by AIDS"] [[Associated Press]], 1999</ref> In November 2007, there was a [[moral panic]] in the United States after widespread reports of jenkem becoming a popular recreational drug in middle and high schools, though the true extent of the practice has since been called into question.<ref name=smokinggun>Collier County Sherriff's Office (September 26, 2007). [http://www.thesmokinggun.com/documents/crime/new-drug-alert Information Bulletin: New Drug - JENKEM.] via ''[[The Smoking Gun]]''</ref><ref name=Salon>{{cite web |url=http://www.salon.com/mwt/feature/2007/11/09/jenkem/?source=rss |title=Smoke this shit |accessdate=2007-11-09 |author=Pietras, Jamie |date=November 9, 2007 |publisher=[[Salon.com]]}}</ref> Several sources reported that the increase in American media coverage was based on a hoax and on faulty [[Internet research]].<ref name=UrbanLegend>{{cite web |url=http://urbanlegends.about.com/library/bl_jenkem.htm |title=Jenkem&nbsp;— Drug Warning |accessdate=2007-11-07 |last=Emery |first=David |date=November 6, 2007 |work=urbanlegends.about.com |publisher=[[About.com]]}}</ref>

==Description==
The name derives from [[Genkem]], a brand of glues which had "become the generic name for all the glues used by [[Inhalant abuse|glue-sniffing]] children" in South Africa, where the drug originated and is most popular on the African continent today.<ref name=cassere2010>Cassere, Di (November 14, 2000). [http://www.iol.co.za/news/south-africa/glue-loses-high-to-save-street-kid-addicts-1.53018 Glue loses high to save street-kid addicts.] ''[[Independent Online (South Africa)|The Independent]]''</ref> In the book ''Children of AIDS: Africa's Orphan Crisis'' by Emma Guest, the making of jenkem is described: "fermented human sewage, scraped from pipes and stored in plastic bags for a week or so, until it gives off numbing, intoxicating fumes."<ref>{{cite book |last=Guest |first=Emma |title=Children of AIDS: Africa's Orphan Crisis |url= |accessdate= |edition=2nd|year=2003 |publisher=Pluto Press |location= |isbn=978-0-7453-2075-5 |oclc= |page=149 |chapter=Falling through the net. A street child's story, Zambia |chapterurl= |quote= }}</ref> The process is similarly described in a 1995 [[Inter Press Service|IPS]] report: "Human [[excreta]] is scooped up from the edges of the sewer ponds in old cans and containers which are covered with a [[polyethylene]] bag and left to stew or ferment for a week."<ref name=IPS/> A 1999 [[BBC]] article refers to "the dark brown sludge, gathering up fistfuls and stuffing it into small plastic bottles. They tap the bottles on the ground, taking care to leave enough room for [[methane]] to form at the top."<ref name=BBC/>

The effects of jenkem inhalation last for around an hour and consist of auditory and visual [[hallucinations]] for some users.<ref name=BBC/> In 1995, one user told a reporter it is "more potent than [[cannabis]]."<ref name=IPS>{{cite news |title=Zambia-Narcotics: 'Huffing and Puffing' to a new High |publisher=[[Inter Press Service]] |date=August 26, 1995}} (reprinted in a Snopes.com [http://www.snopes.com/crime/warnings/jenkem.asp article on jenkem], retrieved December 30, 2007.)</ref> A 1999 report interviewed a user, who said, "With glue, I just hear voices in my head. But with jenkem, I see visions. I see my mother who is dead and I forget about the problems in my life."<ref name=BBC/> Fumito Ichinose, an anesthesia specialist in Boston who conducted a study on the effects of [[hydrogen sulfide]] gas, or "sewer gas," on mice, informed [[Salon (website)|Salon.com]] that holding your breath, choking, or "the inhalation of gases like those produced from jenkem could result in [[hypoxia (medical)|hypoxia]], a lack of oxygen flow to the body that could be alternately euphoric and physically dangerous."<ref name=Salon/>

===2007 to present===
[[File:Jenkem1.jpg|thumb|The Collier County Sheriff Office intelligence bulletin from September 26, 2007 which was later leaked to the media.]]

On September 26, 2007, the Sheriff's Department of [[Collier County, Florida]] issued an internal bulletin about jenkem based on a [[TOTSE]] internet forum post by user "Pickwick," which included purported photos of the manufacture and use.<ref name=smokinggun/> "Pickwick" confessed it was a hoax around the time of the internal bulletin.<ref name=wasahoax>{{cite web |url=http://www.totse.com/community/showthread.php?t=2055429 |title=The jenkem thing was a hoax |last=pickwick |work=TOTSE |publisher=totse.com |accessdate=17 September 2011 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080728042704/http://www.totse.com/community/showthread.php?t=2055429 |archivedate=2008-07-28}}</ref> In November of that year, officials at the [[Drug Enforcement Administration]] said no reports of jenkem use had been confirmed in the United States.<ref name=goldman2007>{{cite news |last=Goldman |first=Russell |title='Jenkem': Stay Alert or Call It a Hoax? |url=http://abcnews.go.com/Health/Drugs/Story?id=3834092&page=1 |format= |publisher=[[ABC News]] |date=November 7, 2007 |accessdate=2007-11-08}}</ref>

After initially classifying stories of Western jenkem use as "undetermined," [[Snopes]] quickly reclassified it as "false." [[About.com]] also concluded reports were "based on faulty [[Internet research]]."<ref name=UrbanLegend/> [[The Smoking Gun]] stated the Collier County alert "may be full of shit".<ref name=NewDrugAlert!-Nov5.2007>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesmokinggun.com/archive/years/2007/1105072jenkem1.html |title=NewDrugAlert!-Nov5.2007 |work= |accessdate=}}</ref> Drug research site [[Erowid]] reported, "the jenkem stories that have been circulating in the U.S. media are almost certainly the strange result of a hoax."<ref>Vaults of Erowid (November 2007). [http://www.erowid.org/ask/ask.php?ID=3113 Ask Erowid: ID 3113.] Retrieved December 30, 2007.</ref>
