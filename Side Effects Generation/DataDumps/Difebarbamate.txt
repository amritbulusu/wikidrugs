'''Difebarbamate''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) is a [[tranquilizer]] of the [[barbiturate]] and [[carbamate]] families which is used in [[Europe]] as a component of a [[combination drug]] [[drug formulation|formulation]] referred to as [[tetrabamate]] ('''Atrium''', '''Sevrium''').<ref name="Index nominum 2000: international drug directory">{{cite book | title = Index nominum 2000: international drug directory | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA333 | accessdate = 26 November 2011 | year = 2000 | publisher = Taylor & Francis US | isbn = 978-3-88763-075-1 | page = 333}}</ref><ref name="WHO2004">{{cite web | url = http://whqlibdoc.who.int/hq/2004/WHO_EDM_QSM_2004.5.pdf | author = World Health Organization | title = The use of stems in the selection of International Nonproprietary Names (INN) for pharmaceutical substance | year = 2004 | format = PDF}}</ref>
