'''Racecadotril''', also known as '''acetorphan''', is an [[antidiarrheal]] drug which acts as a peripherally acting [[enkephalinase inhibitor]].<ref>{{cite journal | journal = Drugs | pmid = 10804038 | volume=59 | issue=4 | title = Racecadotril |date=April 2000 | pages=829–35; discussion 836–7 |vauthors=Matheson AJ, Noble S | doi=10.2165/00003495-200059040-00010}}</ref> Unlike other [[opioid]] medications used to treat diarrhea, which [[Antipropulsive|reduce intestinal motility]], racecadotril has an [[wikt:antisecretory|antisecretory]] effect—it reduces the secretion of water and [[electrolytes]] into the intestine.<ref>{{cite journal|last=Matheson|first=AJ|author2=Noble, S |title=Racecadotril.|journal=Drugs|date=April 2000|volume=59|issue=4|pages=829–35; discussion 836–7|doi=10.2165/00003495-200059040-00010|pmid=10804038}}</ref> It is available in [[France]] (where it was first introduced in ~1990) and other European countries (including [[Germany]], [[Italy]], the [[United Kingdom|UK]], [[Spain]] and the [[Czech Republic]]) as well as most of South America and some [[South East Asia]]n countries (including [[China]], [[India]] and [[Thailand]]), but not in the United States. It is sold under the tradenames '''Hidrasec''' or, in France, '''Tiorfan'''.<ref name = MD/> In Italy it is sold under the tradename '''Tiorfix'''. In India it's available as Redotril.<ref name = MD>{{cite web|title=Racecadotril|work=Martindale: The Complete Drug Reference|publisher=Pharmaceutical Press|location=London, UK|accessdate=6 May 2014|date=13 December 2013|url=http://www.medicinescomplete.com/mc/martindale/current/14912-w.htm|editor=Brayfield, A}}</ref> [[Thiorphan]] is the active metabolite of racecadotril, which exerts the bulk of its inhibitory actions on enkephalinase.<ref name="pmid3015640">{{cite journal | vauthors = Spillantini MG, Geppetti P, Fanciullacci M, Michelacci S, Lecomte JM, Sicuteri F | title = In vivo 'enkephalinase' inhibition by acetorphan in human plasma and CSF | journal = European Journal of Pharmacology | volume = 125 | issue = 1 | pages = 147–50 |date=June 1986 | pmid = 3015640 | doi = 10.1016/0014-2999(86)90094-4| url = http://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/0014-2999(86)90094-4}}</ref>

==Pharmacokinetics==
Racecadotril is rapidly absorbed after oral administration and doses of 30 mg, 100 mg and 300 mg reached&nbsp;''C''max&nbsp;within 60 min. Food does not affect bioavailability of Racecadotril. Racecadotril is rapidly and effectively metabolized to the active metabolite thiorphan which inhibits enkephainase enzyme and exhibits anti-secretory effect.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Eberlin|first=Marion|last2=Mück|first2=Thomas|last3=Michel|first3=Martin C.|date=2012|title=A Comprehensive Review of the Pharmacodynamics, Pharmacokinetics, and Clinical Effects of the Neutral Endopeptidase Inhibitor Racecadotril|url=http://journal.frontiersin.org/article/10.3389/fphar.2012.00093/full|journal=Frontiers in Pharmacology|language=English|volume=3|doi=10.3389/fphar.2012.00093|issn=1663-9812}}</ref>

==Medical uses==
Racecadotril can be used for treatment of acute diarrhea patients and has better tolerability than loperamide.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Fischbach|first=Wolfgang|last2=Andresen|first2=Viola|last3=Eberlin|first3=Marion|last4=Mueck|first4=Tobias|last5=Layer|first5=Peter|date=2016|title=A Comprehensive Comparison of the Efficacy and Tolerability of Racecadotril with Other Treatments of Acute Diarrhea in Adults|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/27790616|journal=Frontiers in Medicine|volume=3|pages=44|doi=10.3389/fmed.2016.00044|issn=2296-858X|pmc=5064048|pmid=27790616}}</ref> Several guidelines have recommended racecadotril use in addition to oral rehydration treatment in children with acute diarrhea.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Eberlin|first=Marion|last2=Mück|first2=Thomas|last3=Michel|first3=Martin C.|date=2012|title=A Comprehensive Review of the Pharmacodynamics, Pharmacokinetics, and Clinical Effects of the Neutral Endopeptidase Inhibitor Racecadotril|url=http://journal.frontiersin.org/article/10.3389/fphar.2012.00093/abstract|journal=Frontiers in Pharmacology|language=English|volume=3|doi=10.3389/fphar.2012.00093|issn=1663-9812}}</ref>
