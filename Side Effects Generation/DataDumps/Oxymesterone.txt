'''Oxymesterone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]]) (brand names '''Anamidol''', '''Balnimax''', '''Oranabol''', '''Sanaboral''', '''Theranabol''', '''Tubil'''), also known as '''methandrostenediolone''', as well as '''4-hydroxy-17α-methyltestosterone''' or '''17α-methylandrost-4-en-4,17β-diol-3-one''', is an [[oral administration|orally active]] [[anabolic-androgenic steroid]] (AAS).<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA923|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=923–}}</ref>

The same compound without the 17α- and 19-methyl groups is called [[oxabolone]].
