'''Properidine''' is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] and the isopropyl analog of [[pethidine]].<ref>{{Cite journal | doi = 10.1039/JR9500001467| title = 302. Synthetic analgesics. Part V. Compounds related to pethidine| journal = Journal of the Chemical Society (Resumed)| pages = 1467| year = 1950| last1 = Morrison | first1 = A. L.| last2 = Rinderknecht | first2 = H.}}</ref>  Properidine is under international control and is listed in the United States under the Controlled Substances Act 1970 as a Schedule I Narcotic with an ACSCN of 9644 and a 2 gramme annual aggregate manufacturing quota as of 2014. The salt in use is the hydrochloride, with a free base conversion ratio of 0.88.<ref>http://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/fed_regs/quotas/2014/fr0825.htm</ref>
