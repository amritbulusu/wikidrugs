'''Enciprazine''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[British Approved Name|BAN]]; '''enciprazine hydrochloride''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]); developmental code names '''WY-48624''', '''D-3112'''), is an [[anxiolytic]] and [[antipsychotic]] of the [[phenylpiperazine]] class which was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA485|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=485–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA109|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=109–}}</ref><ref name="pmid9323305" /><ref name="AdisInsight">{{cite web|url=http://adisinsight.springer.com/drugs/800000720|publisher=adisinsight.springer.com|title=Enciprazine - AdisInsight|accessdate=2017-06-01}}</ref> It shows high [[affinity (pharmacology)|affinity]] for the [[alpha-1 adrenergic receptor|α<sub>1</sub>-adrenergic receptor]] and [[5-HT1A receptor|5-HT<sub>1A</sub> receptor]], among other sites.<ref name="pmid9323305">{{cite journal | vauthors = Matheson GK, Knowles A, Gage D, Michel C, Guthrie D, Bauer C, Blackbourne J, Weinzapfel D | title = Modification of hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenocortical activity by serotonergic agents in the rat | journal = Pharmacology | volume = 55 | issue = 2 | pages = 59–65 | year = 1997 | pmid = 9323305 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid1686125">{{cite journal | vauthors = Scatina JA, Lockhead SR, Cayen MN, Sisenwine SF | title = Metabolic disposition of enciprazine, a non-benzodiazepine anxiolytic drug, in rat, dog and man | journal = Xenobiotica | volume = 21 | issue = 12 | pages = 1591–604 | year = 1991 | pmid = 1686125 | doi = | url = }}</ref><ref name="pmid3178922">{{cite journal | vauthors = Linden M, Helmchen H, Müller-Oerlinghausen B | title = Early phase-II semi double-blind study of the new alkaline propanolamine derivative enciprazine (short communication) | journal = Arzneimittelforschung | volume = 38 | issue = 6 | pages = 814–6 | year = 1988 | pmid = 3178922 | doi = | url = }}</ref> The drug was initially anticipated to produce [[ortho-methoxyphenylpiperazine|''ortho''-methoxyphenylpiperazine]] (oMeOPP), a [[serotonin receptor]] [[agonist]] with high affinity for the 5-HT<sub>1A</sub> receptor, as a significant [[active metabolite]], but subsequent research found this not to be the case.<ref name="pmid1686125" />
