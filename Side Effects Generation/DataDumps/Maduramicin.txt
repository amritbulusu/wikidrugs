'''Maduramicin''' ('''maduramycin''') is an [[antiprotozoal agent]] used in [[veterinary medicine]] to prevent [[coccidiosis]].<ref>[http://www.inspection.gc.ca/animals/feeds/medicating-ingredients/mib/mib-71/eng/1331067351687/1331067403200 Maduramicin Ammonium], [[Canadian Food Inspection Agency]]</ref><ref>{{cite journal |vauthors=McDougald LR, Fuller AL, Mathis GF, Wang GT | title = Efficacy of maduramicin ammonium against coccidiosis in turkeys under laboratory and floor-pen conditions | journal = Avian Diseases | year = 1990 | volume = 34 | issue = 3 | pages = 634–638 | doi = 10.2307/1591256 | pmid = 2241692}}</ref>  It is a natural chemical compound first isolated from the actinomycete ''[[Actinomadura rubra]]''.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=Fleck, W. F. |author2=Strauss, D. G. |author3=Meyer, Jutta |author4=Porstendorfer, Gisela | title = Fermentation, isolation, and biological activity of maduramycin:  a new antibiotic from Actinomadura rubra | journal = Zeitschrift für Allgemeine Mikrobiologie  | year = 1978 | volume = 18 | issue = 6 | pages = 389–98 | doi = 10.1002/jobm.3630180602}}</ref>
