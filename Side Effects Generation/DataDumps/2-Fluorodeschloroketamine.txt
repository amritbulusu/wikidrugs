'''2-Fluorodeschloroketamine''' (also known as '''2-Fl-2'-Oxo-PCM''', '''Fluoroketamine''' and '''2-FDCK''') is a [[Dissociative drug|dissociative]] [[anesthesia|anesthetic]].<ref>{{cite journal | url=http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00397911.2014.885053 | title=Synthesis of 2-(2-Fluorophenyl)-2-methylamino-Cyclohexanone as a New Ketamine Derivative |author1=Abolghasem Moghimi |author2=Siyavash Rahmania |author3=Reza Zare |author4=Morteza Sadeghzadeh | journal=Synthetic Communications | date=2014 | volume=44 | issue=14 | doi=10.1080/00397911.2014.885053}}</ref> It is an analogue of [[ketamine]] where the [[chlorine]] group has been replaced by [[fluorine]].

==Legal status==
2-Fluorodeschloroketamine is illegal in Latvia,<ref>[http://www.vm.gov.lv/images/userfiles/metodiskas_vadlinijas_080914.doc Noteikumi par Latvijā kontrolējamajām narkotiskajām vielām, psihotropajām vielām un prekursoriem]</ref> and is covered by blanket bans in Canada and the UK.
