'''3-Fluoroethamphetamine''' ('''3-FEA''') is a [[stimulant]] [[drug]]  of the [[substituted amphetamine|amphetamine]] class which acts as a releasing agent of the monoamine neurotransmitters [[norepinephrine]], [[dopamine]] and [[serotonin]].

Compared to the unsubstituted [[etilamphetamine|ethylamphetamine]], 3-fluoroethamphetamine is a weaker releaser of noradrenaline, but a stronger releaser of both dopamine and serotonin, and produced the strongest reinforcing effects in animal studies out of a range of 3-substituted ethamphetamine derivatives tested, despite not being the most potent dopamine releaser.<ref>Tessel RE, Woods JH. Structural relationship between meta-substituted N-ethylamphetamines and self-administration in rhesus monkeys. ''Pharmacologist'' 1974;16:142.</ref><ref name="pmid1117418">{{cite journal |author =Tessel RE |author2 =Woods JH |author3 =Counsell RE |author4 =Lu M |title=Structure-activity relationships between meta-substituted N-ethylamphetamines and locomotor activity in mice |journal=The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics |volume=192 |issue=2 |pages=310–8 |date=February 1975 |pmid=1117418 |doi= |url=}}</ref><ref name="pmid1271279">{{cite journal |author =Tessel RE |author2 =Rutledge CO |title=Specificity of release of biogenic amines from isolated rat brain tissue as a function of the meta substituent of N-ethylamphetamine derivatives |journal=The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics |volume=197 |issue=2 |pages=253–62 |date=May 1976 |pmid=1271279 |doi= |url=}}</ref><ref name="pmid417172">{{cite journal |author =Tessel RE |author2 =Woods JH |title=meta Substituted N-ethylamphetamine self injection responding in the rhesus monkey: structure-activity relationships |journal=The Journal of Pharmacology and Experimental Therapeutics |volume=205 |issue=2 |pages=274–81 |date=May 1978 |pmid=417172 |doi= |url=}}</ref>
