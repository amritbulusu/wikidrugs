'''Butidrine''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (brand names '''Betabloc''', '''Butidrate''', '''Recetan'''), or '''butedrine''' or '''butydrine''', also known as '''hydrobutamine''' or '''idrobutamine''', is a [[beta blocker]] related to [[pronethalol]]<ref name="Bristol1986">{{cite book|author=James A. Bristol|title=Cardiovascular drugs|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fd5rAAAAMAAJ|year=1986|publisher=John Wiley & Sons, Incorporated|isbn=978-0-471-09228-5|page=111}}</ref> and [[propranolol]]<ref>{{cite book|title=Drug Metabolism Reviews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=z6EfAQAAMAAJ|year=1972|publisher=Marcel Dekker}}</ref> that was developed in the 1960s.<ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA58|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=58–}}</ref><ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA197|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=197–}}</ref> Similarly to certain other beta blockers, butidrine also possesses [[local anesthetic]] properties.<ref name="Charlier1971">{{cite book|author=Robert Charlier|title=Antianginal drugs: pathophysiological, haemodynamic, methodological, pharmacological, biochemical and clinical basis for their use in human therapeutics|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=s5UKAQAAMAAJ|year=1971|publisher=Springer-Verlag|isbn=978-3-540-05365-1}}</ref>
