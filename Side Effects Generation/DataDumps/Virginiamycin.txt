'''Virginiamycin''' is a [[streptogramin]] antibiotic similar to [[pristinamycin]] and [[quinupristin/dalfopristin]]. It is a combination of [[pristinamycin IIA]] (virginiamycin M1) and [[virginiamycin S1]].  Virginiamycin is used in the fuel ethanol industry to prevent microbial contamination.<ref>[http://www.fathom.com/course/21701753/session4.html University of Michigan: Resistant Microbes, Antibiotic Abuse, and the Threat to Public Health]</ref>  It is also used in agriculture, specifically in livestock, to accelerate the growth of the animals and to prevent and treat infections. <ref name="msnbc">{{cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/34614380/ns/health-infectious_diseases/|title=Drug-resistant infections lurk in the meat we eat - Infectious Diseases - msnbc.com|accessdate=December 29, 2009}}</ref> Antibiotics also save as much as 30% in feed costs among young swine, although the savings fade as pigs get older, according to a USDA study.<ref name="msnbc"/>
