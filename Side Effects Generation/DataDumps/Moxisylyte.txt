'''Moxisylyte''', also known as '''thymoxamine''', is a drug used in [[urology]] for the treatment of [[erectile dysfunction]].<ref>British National Formulary 69, Chapter2.6.4, page 142</ref> It is an [[alpha-1 adrenergic|α<sub>1</sub>-adrenergic]] [[alpha blocker|antagonist]].<ref name="pmid10699651">{{cite journal  |vauthors=Sakakibara R, Hattori T, Uchiyama T, etal |title=Are alpha-blockers involved in lower urinary tract dysfunction in multiple system atrophy? A comparison of prazosin and moxisylyte |journal=J. Auton. Nerv. Syst. |volume=79 |issue=2–3 |pages=191–5 |date=March 2000 |pmid=10699651 |doi= 10.1016/S0165-1838(99)00105-8|url=http://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0165183899001058}}</ref>
In the United Kingdom, Moxisylte is marketed as Opilon (Archimedes Pharma UK Ltd) and is used for the short-term treatment of [[primary Reynaud's syndrome.]] This is a condition where the fingers and toes become discoloured and is triggered by responses to cold, or emotional distress. Opilon tablets help by improving blood circulation to the extremities.<ref>British National Formulary 70, page 205</ref><ref>https://www.medicines.org.uk/emc/medicine/18240</ref>
