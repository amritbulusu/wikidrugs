'''Nexeridine'''<ref>US 3974157 '1-(Amino-alkyl)-2-aryl-cyclohexane alcohols and esters' Grant</ref> ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), or as '''nexeridine hydrochloride''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]) (code name '''Compound 673-082'''), is an [[opioid]] [[analgesic]] with a similar [[chemical structure|structure]] to those of [[pethidine]] and [[tramadol]].<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&pg=PA1417|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=1417–}}</ref><ref name="Kar2005">{{cite book|author=Ashutosh Kar|title=Medicinal Chemistry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=07g30rxCA0EC&pg=PA268|date=1 January 2005|publisher=New Age International|isbn=978-81-224-1565-0|pages=268–269}}</ref><ref name="Special Libraries Association1975">{{cite book|title=Unlisted Drugs, Volumes 27-28|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9xZtAAAAMAAJ|year=1975|publisher=Pharmaceutical Section, Special Libraries Association.}}</ref> It was [[chemical synthesis|synthesized]] and [[assay]]ed in 1975 but was never marketed.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996" /> The active isomer is (1R,2S)-1-[(2R)-1-(Dimethylamino)-2-propanyl]-2-phenylcyclohexyl acetate hydrochloride (1:1).<ref>http://www.chemspider.com/Chemical-Structure.34995858.html</ref>
