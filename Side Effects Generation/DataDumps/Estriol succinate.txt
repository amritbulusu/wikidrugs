'''Estriol succinate''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (brand names '''Sinapause''', '''Styptanon''', '''Synapasa''', '''Synapausa''', '''Synapause''', '''Hemostyptanon''', '''Orgastyptin''', '''Ovestin''', '''Evalon'''), or '''oestriol succinate''' ([[British Approved Name|BAN]]), also known as '''estriol disuccinate''' or '''estriol hemisuccinate''', as well as '''estriol 16α,17β-di(hydrogen succinate)''', is a [[semisynthetic]], [[steroid]]al [[estrogen]] that is marketed in [[Europe]], [[Hong Kong]], and [[Mexico]] and was introduced in 1966.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=RA1-PA129|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|page=899}}</ref><ref name="IndexNominum2000">{{cite book|title=Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA407|date=January 2000|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-3-88763-075-1|pages=407–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA114|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=114–}}</ref> It is an [[estrogen ester]], specifically, an [[ester]] of [[estriol]].<ref name="Elks2014" /> The drug is described as a weak estrogen (relative to [[estradiol valerate]], which is described as a strong estrogen).<ref name="CutlerGarcía1984">{{cite book|author1=Winnifred Berg Cutler|author2=Celso-Ramón García|title=The medical management of menopause and premenopause: their endocrinologic basis|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=79lsAAAAMAAJ|year=1984|publisher=Lippincott Williams & Wilkins|isbn=978-0-397-50631-6|page=31}}</ref> It was introduced in 1966 and is used in the treatment of [[menopause|menopausal]] symptoms.<ref name="Publishing2013">{{cite book|author=William Andrew Publishing|title=Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Encyclopedia, 3rd Edition|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_J2ti4EkYpkC&pg=PA1481|date=22 October 2013|publisher=Elsevier|isbn=978-0-8155-1856-3|pages=1481–}}</ref> The drug is also available in [[sodium]] [[salt (chemistry)|salt]] form as '''oestriol sodium succinate''' ([[British Approved Name|BAN]]) (brand names '''Pausan''', '''Styptanon''').<ref name="Elks2014" /><ref name="IndexNominum2000" />
