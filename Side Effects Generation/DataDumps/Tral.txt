'''Tral''' ({{lang-ks|{{nq|ترال}}}}) is a town and a [[notified area committee]] in [[Pulwama district]] of [[Jammu and Kashmir]], [[India]], situated 10&nbsp;kms away from NH1(via Nowdal route).

== Geography ==
Tral is located at {{Coord|33.93|N|75.1|E}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/12/Tral.html|title=Maps, Weather, and Airports for Tral, India|author=|date=|work=fallingrain.com}}</ref>  It has an average elevation of 1662&nbsp;metres (5452&nbsp;ft). The place is having an average area of about 110&nbsp;km².

Tral is situated at the edge of plateau divided into upper and lower [[mohalla]]s. The valley is surrounded by the snow peak mountains also famous for its  natural springs, dense forests and beautiful villages. Famous picnic spots in the vicinity include Shikargah, Aripal and Hajan. The area is known for its 82 springs, including [[Hot spring|hot water spring]]s and springs remaining hot in winter and cold in summer.

The houses that are built on this range are mostly made up of bricks (some sun dried and some baked). The roofs are usually made of tin while some are occasionally thatched. Tral is shaded by fine trees. The area possess enormous number of springs located in its 82 villages. Plateau lands on the east side of the town is bare and dry but the slope to the west and to its beneath has a large mass of rice cultivation.

== Demographics ==
{{As of|2011}} India [[census]],<ref name=DemoCensus>{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.gov.in/pca/SearchDetails.aspx?Id=8145|website=www.censusindia.gov.in|publisher=Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India|accessdate=27 July 2014|title=Census of India}}</ref> Tral had a population of 17,844 with 9,944 males constituting 55.7% of the population and 7,900 females constituting 44.3% of the population.

==Education==
[[Tral]] is considered one of the most educated towns in the state of [[Jammu and Kashmir]]. it's leading in the overall literacy rate among the other Municipal Committees in the [[Kashmir]] zone. Literacy rate of [[Tral]] is 85.92 %, higher than state average of 67.16 %. In Tral, Male literacy is around 92.85 % while female literacy rate is 65.62 %

== Etymology ==
The name ''Tral'' is said to originate from ''trī lāl'' ({{nq|تریہ لال}}), meaning "Three Pearls".

== Security situation ==
Tral is considered the most volatile area in Jammu & Kashmir and a militancy hotbed. [[Burhan Muzaffar Wani]], former commander of [[Hizbul Mujahideen]], hailed from Tral.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.dailyo.in/politics/kashmir-indiapak-hurriyat-burhan-muzaffar-wani-hizb-ul-mujahideen-syed-ali-shah-geelani/story/1/7055.html| title=Why south Kashmir is becoming the new hotbed of militancy| author=| date=| work=dailyo.in}}</ref>  Hizbul Mujahideen commander [[Sabzar Bhat]], successor of Burhan Wani hailing from Ratsuna Tral was also killed in Soimoh Tral, by Indian security forces sparking days of unrest.<ref>{{cite news |publisher=[[Al Jazeera]] |quote=Sabzar Ahmad Bhat, a senior leader of the Hizbul Mujahideen group, was killed overnight by government troops in Tral area, some 40km south of Kashmir's capital, Srinagar, police said. |title=Top rebel commander Sabzar Ahmad Bhat killed in Kashmir |date=May 2017 |url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2017/05/top-rebel-commander-sabzar-ahmad-bhat-killed-kashmir-170527093919071.html}}</ref>
