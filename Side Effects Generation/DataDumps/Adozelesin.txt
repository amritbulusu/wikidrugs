'''Adozelesin''' is an experimental antitumor drug of the [[duocarmycin]] class.<ref>{{cite journal|pmid=11325853|year=2001|last1=Wang|first1=Y|last2=Beerman|first2=TA|last3=Kowalski|first3=D|title=Antitumor drug adozelesin differentially affects active and silent origins of DNA replication in yeast checkpoint kinase mutants|volume=61|issue=9|pages=3787–94|journal=Cancer Research}}</ref> It binds to and alkylates DNA, resulting in a reduction of both cellular and simian virus 40 ([[SV40]]) DNA replication which ultimately reduces the rate of cancer growth.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Liu|first1=J.-S.|last2=Kuo|first2=S.-R.|last3=McHugh|first3=M. M.|last4=Beerman|first4=T. A.|last5=Melendy|first5=T.|title=Adozelesin Triggers DNA Damage Response Pathways and Arrests SV40 DNA Replication through Replication Protein A Inactivation|journal=Journal of Biological Chemistry|date=14 January 2000|volume=275|issue=2|pages=1391–1397|doi=10.1074/jbc.275.2.1391}}</ref>
