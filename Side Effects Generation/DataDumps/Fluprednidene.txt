'''Fluprednidene''', also known as '''fluprednylidene''', is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]] [[glucocorticoid]] [[corticosteroid]] which was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA570|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=570–}}</ref><ref name="IndexNominum2000">{{cite book|title=Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA463|year=2000|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-3-88763-075-1|pages=463–}}</ref><ref name="Challener2001">{{cite book|author=Cynthia A. Challener|title=Chiral Drugs|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hyhtAAAAMAAJ|date=1 December 2001|publisher=Wiley|isbn=978-0-566-08411-9|page=490}}</ref> An [[acetate]] [[ester]] of fluprednidene, [[fluprednidene acetate]], in contrast, has been marketed.<ref name="Elks2014" /><ref name="IndexNominum2000" /><ref name="Challener2001" />
