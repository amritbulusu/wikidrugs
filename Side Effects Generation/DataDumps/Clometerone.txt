'''Clometerone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (developmental code name '''L-38000'''), or '''clometherone''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]), also known as '''6α-chloro-16α-methylprogesterone''', is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]] [[pregnane]] [[steroid]] and [[derivative (chemistry)|derivative]] of [[progesterone]] which was reported in 1962 and is described as an [[antiandrogen]] and [[antiestrogen]] but was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA298|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=298–}}</ref><ref name="StraussPochi1970">{{cite journal | last1 = Strauss | first1 = John S. | last2 = Pochi | first2 = Peter E. | title = Assay of anti-androgens in man by the sebaceous gland response | journal = British Journal of Dermatology | volume = 82 | issue = s6 | year = 1970 | page = 33 | issn = 0007-0963 | doi = 10.1111/j.1365-2133.1970.tb08003.x}}</ref>
