'''Prospidium chloride''' ('''prospidine''') is a drug with cytostatic (alkylating) and anti-inflammatory properties.  It has been studied for the treatment of [[rheumatoid arthritis]].<ref>{{cite journal | doi = 10.1007/BF02229866| title = Prospidine versus methotrexate pulse in highly active rheumatoid arthritis: A controlled 6-month clinical trial| journal = Clinical Rheumatology| volume = 13| pages = 54| year = 1994| last1 = Benenson| first1 = E. V.| last2 = Timina| first2 = O. B.}}</ref>

Chemically, it is a [[spiro compound]].
