'''Haloprogesterone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], [[United States Adopted Name|USAN]], brand name '''Prohalone'''), also known as '''aloprogesterone''' or '''6α-fluoro-17α-bromoprogesterone''', is a [[halogen]]ated [[chemical derivative|derivative]] of [[progesterone]] and [[steroid]]al [[progestin]] which was previously marketed by [[Ayerst Laboratories]] but is no longer available.<ref name="GanellinTriggle1996">{{cite book|author1=C.R. Ganellin|author2=David J. Triggle|title=Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&pg=PA999|date=21 November 1996|publisher=CRC Press|isbn=978-0-412-46630-4|pages=999–}}</ref><ref name="Association1976">{{cite book|author=American Pharmaceutical Association|title=APhA drug names: an index of drug names|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IwdtAAAAMAAJ|year=1976|publisher=American Pharmaceutical Association}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=United States Adopted Names (USAN).|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xvlsAAAAMAAJ|year=1968|publisher=United States Pharmacopeial Convention.|page=715}}</ref>
