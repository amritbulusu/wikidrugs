'''Haloxazolam''' (marketed in Japan under the brand name '''Somelin'''), is a drug which is a [[benzodiazepine]] derivative.<ref>{{cite journal |pmid=8832439 |year=1996 |last1=Tanaka |first1=E |last2=Terada |last3=Misawa |last4=Wakasugi |title=Simultaneous determination of twelve benzodiazepines in human serum using a new reversed-phase chromatographic column on a 2-microns porous microspherical silica gel |volume=682 |issue=1 |pages=173–8 |journal=Journal of Chromatography B |doi=10.1016/0378-4347(96)00121-1 |first2=M |first3=S |first4=C }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.non-benzodiazepines.org.uk/benzodiazepine-names.html| title=Benzodiazepine Names| accessdate=2009-04-05| publisher=non-benzodiazepines.org.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |pmid=10022210 |year=1999 |last1=Guan |first1=F |last2=Seno |last3=Ishii |last4=Watanabe |last5=Kumazawa |last6=Hattori |last7=Suzuki |title=Solid-phase microextraction and GC-ECD of benzophenones for detection of benzodiazepines in urine |volume=23 |issue=1 |pages=54–61 |journal=Journal of analytical toxicology |first2=H |first3=A |first4=K |first5=T |first6=H |first7=O |doi=10.1093/jat/23.1.54}}</ref> It has similar [[hypnotic]] properties as the benzodiazepine drugs [[triazolam]], [[temazepam]], and [[flunitrazepam]] and as such is indicated for the treatment [[insomnia]].<ref>{{cite journal |pmid=12519461 |year=2003 |last1=Tan |first1=X |last2=Uchida |last3=Matsuura |last4=Nishihara |last5=Kojima |title=Long-, intermediate- and short-acting benzodiazepine effects on human sleep EEG spectra |volume=57 |issue=1 |pages=97–104 |journal=Psychiatry and clinical neurosciences |doi=10.1046/j.1440-1819.2003.01085.x |first2=S |first3=M |first4=K |first5=T }}</ref> A study in cats comparing [[estazolam]] and haloxazolam found that haloxazolam only affects [[gamma motor neurons]], whereas estazolam affects both alpha and gamma motor neurons.<ref>{{cite journal |pmid=6139494 |year=1983 |last1=Sakai |first1=Y |title=Comparative study on the effects of haloxazolam and estazolam, new sleep inducing drugs, on the alpha- and gamma-motor systems. |volume=33 |issue=5 |pages=1017–25 |journal=Japanese journal of pharmacology |doi=10.1254/jjp.33.1017 }}</ref>
