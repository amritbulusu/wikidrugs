'''Demexiptiline''' (brand names '''Deparon''', '''Tinoran''') is a [[tricyclic antidepressant]] (TCA) used in [[France]] for the treatment of [[Major depressive disorder|depression]].<ref name="isbn3-88763-075-0">{{cite book | author = Swiss Pharmaceutical Society | title = Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory (Book with CD-ROM) | publisher = Medpharm Scientific Publishers | location = Boca Raton | year = 2000 | page = 301 | isbn = 3-88763-075-0 | oclc = | doi = | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&lpg=PA1267&dq=demexiptiline&as_brr=3&pg=PA301#v=onepage&q=&f=false}}</ref><ref name="isbn0-412-46630-9">{{cite book | author = David J. Triggle | title = Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents | publisher = Chapman & Hall/CRC | location = Boca Raton | year = 1996 | page = 583 | isbn = 0-412-46630-9 | oclc = | doi = | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=DeX7jgInYFMC&lpg=RA1-PA583&dq=demexiptiline&as_brr=3&pg=RA1-PA583#v=onepage&q=&f=false}}</ref> It acts primarily as a [[norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor]] similarly to [[desipramine]].<ref name="pmid8370568">{{cite journal | vauthors = Teste JF, Pelsy-Johann I, Decelle T, Boulu RG | title = Anti-immobility activity of different antidepressant drugs using the tail suspension test in normal or reserpinized mice | journal = Fundamental & Clinical Pharmacology | volume = 7 | issue = 5 | pages = 219–26 | year = 1993 | pmid = 8370568 | doi = 10.1111/j.1472-8206.1993.tb00235.x| url = }}</ref>
