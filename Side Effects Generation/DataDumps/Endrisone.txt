'''Endrisone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (brand name '''Aldrisone'''), or '''endrysone''' ([[United States Adopted Name|USAN]]), is a [[synthetic compound|synthetic]], [[steroid]]al [[glucocorticoid]] which is or has been marketed in [[Italy]] by {{abbr|SIFI|Società Industria Farmaceutica Italiana}}.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA486|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=486–}}</ref><ref name="IndexNominum2000">{{cite book|title=Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA386|date=January 2000|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-3-88763-075-1|pages=386–}}</ref><ref name="MortonHall2012">{{cite book|author1=I.K. Morton|author2=Judith M. Hall|title=Concise Dictionary of Pharmacological Agents: Properties and Synonyms|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tsjrCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA110|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-4439-1|pages=110–}}</ref> It is used as a [[topical medication|topical]] and [[ophthalmology|ophthalmic]] [[anti-inflammatory drug]] in the treatment of [[skin disorder|skin]] and [[eye condition]]s, respectively.<ref name="Elks2014" /><ref name="MortonHall2012" />
