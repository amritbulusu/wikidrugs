'''Tampramine''' ('''AHR-9,377''') is a [[tricyclic antidepressant]] (TCA) which was developed in the 1980s but was never marketed.<ref name="pmid6548381"/><ref name="pmid3936083">{{cite journal |vauthors=O'Donnell JM, Seiden LS | title = Effect of the experimental antidepressant AHR-9377 on performance during differential reinforcement of low response rate | journal = Psychopharmacology | volume = 87 | issue = 3 | pages = 283–5 | year = 1985 | pmid = 3936083 | doi = 10.1007/BF00432708| url = }}</ref> Despite being a TCA, it acts as a [[binding selectivity|selective]] [[norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor]] and has negligible [[affinity (pharmacology)|affinity]] for [[adrenergic]], [[histaminergic]], and [[muscarinic]] [[receptor (biochemistry)|receptor]]s.<ref name="pmid6548381">{{cite journal |vauthors=Kinnier WJ, Tabor RD, Norrell LY | title = Neurochemical properties of AHR-9377: a novel inhibitor of norepinephrine reuptake | journal = [[Biochemical Pharmacology (journal)|Biochemical Pharmacology]] | volume = 33 | issue = 19 | pages = 3001–5 |date=October 1984 | pmid = 6548381 | doi = 10.1016/0006-2952(84)90600-2| url = http://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/0006-2952(84)90600-2}}</ref> It was found to be effective in the [[forced swim test]] (FST) model of [[major depression|depression]] in animal studies but is not known to have ever been trialed in humans.<ref name="pmid3936083"/>
