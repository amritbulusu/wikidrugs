'''Cismadinone''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]), also known as '''6α-chloro-17α-hydroxypregna-1,4-diene-3,20-dione''' or '''6α-chloro-δ<sup>1</sup>-dehydro-17α-hydroxyprogesterone''', is a [[steroid]]al [[progestin]] closely related to the [[17α-hydroxyprogesterone]] derivatives that was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=RA1-PA73|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|page=284}}</ref><ref name="WHOINNStems2013">{{citation | author = World Health Organization | title = The use of stems in the selection of International Nonproprietary Names (INN) for pharmaceutical substances | year = 2013 | page = 104 | url = http://apps.who.int/medicinedocs/collect/medicinedocs/pdf/s4895e/s4895e.pdf}}</ref> An [[acetyl]]ated form, [[cismadinone acetate]], also exists, but similarly to cismadinone, was never marketed.<ref name="Elks2014" />
