'''Octamoxin''' (trade names '''Ximaol''', '''Nimaol'''), also known as '''2-octylhydrazine''', is an [[irreversible inhibition|irreversible]] and [[binding selectivity|nonselective]] [[monoamine oxidase inhibitor]] (MAOI) of the [[hydrazine]] class that was used as an [[antidepressant]] in the 1960s but is now no longer marketed.<ref name="urlDictionary of pharmacological agents - Google Books">{{cite web | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=A0THacd46ZsC&lpg=PA1323&ots=IIjkfFzga1&dq=octamoxin%20ximaol&pg=PA1323#v=onepage&q=octamoxin%20ximaol&f=false | title = Dictionary of pharmacological agents - Google Books | format = | work = | accessdate = }}</ref><ref name="url13-06781.
