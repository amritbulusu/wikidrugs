'''Tocofersolan''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) or '''tocophersolan''' is a synthetic water-soluble version of [[vitamin E]].  Natural forms of vitamin E are fat soluble, but not water-soluble.  Tocofersolan is [[polyethylene glycol]] derivative of [[alpha-Tocopherol|α-tocopherol]] that enables water solubility.  

Tocofersolan is used as a vitamin E supplement or to treat vitamin E deficiency in individuals who cannot absorb fats due to disease.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.webmd.com/drugs/drug-149234-Tocophersolan+Oral.aspx?drugid=149234&drugname=Tocophersolan+Oral|title=Tocophersolan Oral|publisher=[[WebMD]]}}</ref>
On 24 July 2009 the [[European Medicines Agency]] approved tocofersolan under the trade name '''Vedrop''' 50&nbsp;mg/ml oral solution for the treatment of vitamin E deficiency due to digestive [[malabsorption]] in [[paediatric]] patients suffering from [[congenital]] or hereditary chronic [[cholestasis]], from birth (in term newborns) to 16 or 18 years of age (depending on the region).<ref>{{cite web|title=''Vedrop Summary of Product Characteristics|url=http://www.ema.europa.eu/humandocs/PDFs/EPAR/vedrop/emea-combined-h920en.pdf|publisher=[[European Medicines Agency]]}}</ref>

Tocofersolan is also used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals as an [[antioxidant]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Handbook of Preservatives|last1=Ash|first1=Michael|last2=Ash|first2=Irene|p=568|isbn=1-890595-66-7}}</ref>
