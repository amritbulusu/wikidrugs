'''Homosalate''' is an [[organic compound]] used in some [[sunscreen]]s.  It is an [[ester]] formed from [[salicylic acid]] and 3,3,5-trimethylcyclohexanol, a derivative of [[cyclohexanol]].  Contained in 45% of U.S. sunscreens, it is used as a chemical UV filter.<ref>[http://chem.sis.nlm.nih.gov/chemidplus/rn/118-56-9 Homosalate], ChemIDplus</ref> The salicylic acid portion of the molecule absorbs [[ultraviolet]] rays with a wavelength from 295&nbsp;nm to 315&nbsp;nm, protecting the skin from sun damage.  The [[hydrophobic]] cyclohexanol portion provides greasiness that prevents it from dissolving in water.
