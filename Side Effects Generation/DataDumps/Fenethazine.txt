'''Fenethazine''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]]) (brand names '''Anergen''', '''Contralergial''', '''Ethysine''', '''Etisine''', '''Lisergan''', '''Lysergan'''; former developmental code names '''RP-3015''', '''SC-1627''', '''WY-1143'''), or '''phenethazine''', is a first-generation [[antihistamine]] of the [[phenothiazine]] group.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA434|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=434–}}</ref> [[Promethazine]], and subsequently [[chlorpromazine]], were derived from fenethazine.<ref name="Dörwald2013">{{cite book|author=Florencio Zaragoza Dörwald|title=Lead Optimization for Medicinal Chemists: Pharmacokinetic Properties of Functional Groups and Organic Compounds|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YTeY9ZEfNccC&pg=PA301|date=4 February 2013|publisher=John Wiley & Sons|isbn=978-3-527-64565-7|pages=301–}}</ref> Fenethazine, in turn, was derived from [[phenbenzamine]].<ref name="Sneader2005">{{cite book|author=Walter Sneader|title=Drug Discovery: A History|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Cb6BOkj9fK4C&pg=PA404|date=23 June 2005|publisher=John Wiley & Sons|isbn=978-0-471-89979-2|pages=404–}}</ref>
