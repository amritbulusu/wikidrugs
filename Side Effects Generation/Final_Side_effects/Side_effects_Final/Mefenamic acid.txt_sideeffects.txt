Mefenamic acid is recommended to be taken with food.<ref>{{ cite web | url = http://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/druginfo/meds/a681028.html#side-effects | publisher = National Institutes of Health | work = Medline Plus | title = Side effects for Mefenamic Acid }}</ref>

 


 
Known mild [[Adverse drug reaction|side effects]] of mefenamic acid include [[headache]]s, nervousness, and [[vomiting]].  Serious side effects may include [[diarrhea]], [[hematemesis]] (vomiting blood), [[hematuria]] (blood in urine), blurred vision, skin rash, itching and swelling, sore throat and [[fever]].<ref name=Aronson>Jeffrey K. Aronson. Meyler's Side Effects of Analgesics and Anti-inflammatory Drugs. Elsevier, 2009 {{ISBN|9780080932941}}</ref>{{rp|334}}  It has been associated with acute liver damage.<ref name=LiverTox/>

 


 
In 2008 the US label was updated with a warning concerning a risk of premature closure of the [[ductus arteriosus]] in pregnancy.<ref>FDA March 2008 [http://www.fda.gov/safety/medwatch/safetyinformation/safety-relateddruglabelingchanges/ucm116459.htm FDA advisory]</ref>
