Sulfacetamide 10% topical lotion, sold under the brand name Klaron or Ovace, is approved for the treatment of [[acne]] and [[seborrheic dermatitis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/mtm/klaron.html|title=Klaron medical facts from Drugs.com|publisher=}}</ref> When combined with [[sulfur]], it is sold under the brand names [[Plexion]], Clenia, Prascion, and Avar, which contain 10% sulfacetamide and 5% sulfur.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/cdi/avar-cream.html|title=Avar cream: Indications, Side Effects, Warnings - Drugs.com|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/mtm/plexion.html|title=Plexion medical facts from Drugs.com|publisher=}}</ref><ref>http://www.walgreens.com/marketing/library/finddrug/druginfo1.html;jsessionid=PbrFt7xeQx07A7CTF6HcFA**.p_dotcom34?particularDrug=Prascion&id=645494</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/pro/clenia-cream.html|title=Clenia Cream - FDA prescribing information, side effects and uses|publisher=}}</ref>

 
Sulfacetamide is a sulfonamide antibiotic, that is used as a cream to treat skin infections and as eye drops to treat eye infections. On the skin it is used to treat acne and [[Seborrhoeic dermatitis|seborrheic dermatitis]].<ref name=":4" /> In cream form it is used to treat bacterial infections on the skin. It can also be used orally to treat [[urinary tract infection]]s.<ref name=":0" /> It kills the bacteria by restricting the production of folic acid, which is essential for bacterial growth.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=http://www.drugs.com/cdi/sulfacetamide-cream.html|title=Sulfacetamide cream: Indications, Side Effects, Warnings - Drugs.com|website=www.drugs.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> It mainly inhibits the multiplication of bacteria as it acts in a competitive inhibitor.

 
== Side effects ==

 
The most common side effects are irritation, stinging or burning of the skin. Other side effects include nausea, vomiting, dizziness, tiredness and headache.<ref name=":0" /> There are however also severe side effects including severe allergic reactions, like (nettle) rash, itch, tightness in chest, difficult breathing and swelling in either the face, mouth, lips or tongue. Other severe side effects include bloody or severe diarrhea, fever, joint pain, red, blistered or swollen skin and stomach pain.<ref name=":8" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.healthline.com/drugs/sulfacetamide/topical-gel#SideEffects2|title=Sulfacetamide: Side Effects, Dosage, Uses|website=www.healthline.com|access-date=2016-03-09}}</ref> In the eye, it can cause [[conjunctivitis]]. There are also life-threatening conditions which can be produced by the antibiotic, like [[Stevens–Johnson syndrome|Stevens Johnson syndrome]] and [[Erythema multiforme]].<ref name=":5" /> Higher exposure can also cause unconsciousness.<ref name=":0" /> Another case showed that sulfacetamide eyedrops caused the life-threatening skin condition [[toxic epidermal necrolysis]] (TEN).<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Byrom|first=Lisa|last2=Zappala|first2=Tania|last3=Muir|first3=Jim|date=2013-05-01|title=Toxic epidermal necrolysis caused by over the counter eyedrops|url=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1440-0960.2012.00936.x/abstract|journal=Australasian Journal of Dermatology|language=en|volume=54|issue=2|pages=144–146|doi=10.1111/j.1440-0960.2012.00936.x|issn=1440-0960}}</ref>  These are however not all side effects. For more information the health care provider can be contacted.

 
There are no known carcinogenic and mutagenic effects. It has a moderate toxicity according to the Chemwatch hazard ratings.<ref name=":10">{{cite web|publisher=CHEMWATCH|year=2011|title=Sulfacetamide|url=http://datasheets.scbt.com/sds/AGHS/EN/sc-251080.pdf}}</ref>
