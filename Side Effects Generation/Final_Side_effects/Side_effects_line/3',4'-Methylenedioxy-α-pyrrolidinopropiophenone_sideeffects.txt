'''3',4'-Methylenedioxy-α-pyrrolidinopropiophenone''' ('''MDPPP''') is a [[stimulant]] [[designer drug]]. It was sold in Germany in the late 1990s and early 2000s as an ingredient in imitation [[Methylenedioxymethamphetamine|ecstasy (MDMA)]] pills.<ref name="Fritschi" /> It shares a similar chemical structure with [[Alpha-Pyrrolidinopropiophenone|α-PPP]] and [[Methylenedioxypyrovalerone|MDPV]],<ref name="Maurer" /><ref name="Staack" /><ref name="Springer" /> and has been shown to have reinforcing effects in rats.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Gannon|first1=BM|last2=Galindo|first2=KI|last3=Mesmin|first3=MP|last4=Sulima|first4=A|last5=Rice|first5=KC|last6=Collins|first6=GT|title=Relative reinforcing effects of second-generation synthetic cathinones: Acquisition of self-administration and fixed ratio dose-response curves in rats.|journal=Neuropharmacology|date=12 August 2017|doi=10.1016/j.neuropharm.2017.08.018|pmid=28811192}}</ref>
