'''Sarmazenil''' ('''Ro15-3505''') is a drug from the [[benzodiazepine]] family. It acts as a partial [[inverse agonist]] of [[benzodiazepine receptor]]s,<ref>{{ cite journal |author1=López-Romero B |author2=Evrard G |author3=Durant F |author4=Sevrin M |author5=George P | title = Molecular structure and stereoelectronic properties of sarmazenil--a weak inverse agonist at the omega modulatory sites (benzodiazepine receptors): comparison with bretazenil and flumazenil | journal = Bioorganic and Medicinal Chemistry | year = 1998 | volume = 6 | issue = 10 | pages = 1745–57 | pmid = 9839004 | doi = 10.1016/S0968-0896(98)00117-5 }}</ref> meaning that it causes the opposite effects to most benzodiazepine drugs, and instead acts as an [[anxiogenic]] and [[convulsant]]. It is used in [[veterinary medicine]] to reverse the effects of benzodiazepine sedative drugs in order to rapidly re-awaken anesthetized animals.<ref>{{ cite journal |author1=Henke J |author2=Roberts U |author3=Otto K |author4=Lendl C |author5=Matis U |author6=Brill T |author7=Erhardt W | title = Clinical investigations of an i.m. combination anesthesia with fentanyl / climazolam / xylazine and postoperative i.v. antagonism with naloxone / sarmazenil / yohimbine in guinea pigs | language = German | journal = Tierärztliche Praxis | year = 1996 | volume = 24 | issue = 1 | pages = 85–7 | pmid = 8720962 }}</ref><ref>{{ cite journal |author1=Janovsky M |author2=Tataruch F |author3=Ambuehl M |author4=Giacometti M | title = A Zoletil-Rompun mixture as an alternative to the use of opioids for immobilization of feral red deer | journal = Journal of Wildlife Diseases | year = 2000 | volume = 36 | issue = 4 | pages = 663–9 | pmid = 11085427 | doi = 10.7589/0090-3558-36.4.663 }}</ref><ref>{{ cite journal |author1=Walzer C |author2=Huber C | title = Partial antagonism of tiletamine-zolazepam anesthesia in cheetah | journal = Journal of Wildlife Diseases | year = 2002 | volume = 38 | issue = 2 | pages = 468–72 | pmid = 12038151 | doi = 10.7589/0090-3558-38.2.468 }}</ref>
