'''Epinastine''' (brand names '''Alesion''', '''Elestat''', '''Purivist''', '''Relestat''') is a second-generation [[antihistamine]] and [[mast cell]] stabilizer that is used in [[eye drop]]s to treat [[allergic conjunctivitis]]. It is produced by [[Allergan]] and marketed by Inspire in the United States.<ref>{{cite journal|pmid=19630694|year=2009|last1=Pradhan|first1=S|last2=Abhishek|first2=K|last3=Mah|first3=F|title=Epinastine: topical ophthalmic second generation antihistamine without significant systemic side effects|volume=5|issue=9|pages=1135–40|doi=10.1517/17425250903117284|journal=Expert opinion on drug metabolism & toxicology}}</ref> It is highly [[binding selectivity|selective]] for the [[H1 receptor|H<sub>1</sub> receptor]] and does not cross the [[blood-brain-barrier]].<ref name="pmid1972625">{{cite journal |vauthors=Walther G, Daniel H, Bechtel WD, Brandt K | title = New tetracyclic guanidine derivatives with H1-antihistaminic properties.
