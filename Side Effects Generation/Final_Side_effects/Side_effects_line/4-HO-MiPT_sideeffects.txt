'''4-HO-MiPT''' ('''miprocin''', '''4-hydroxy-''N''-methyl-''N''-isopropyltryptamine''') is a [[Organic compound#Synthetic compounds|synthetic]] [[Substitution reaction#Substituted compounds|substituted]] [[aromatic]] [[Chemical compound|compound]] and a lesser-known [[psychedelic drug|psychedelic]] [[tryptamine]]. It is thought to be a [[serotonergic psychedelic]], similar to [[magic mushrooms]], [[LSD]] and [[mescaline]].{{cn|date=November 2013}} Its molecular structure and pharmacological effects somewhat resemble those of the tryptamine [[psilocin]], which is the primary psychoactive chemical in [[magic mushrooms]].
