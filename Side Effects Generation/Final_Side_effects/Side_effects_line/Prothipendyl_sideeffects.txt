'''Prothipendyl''' (brand names '''Dominal''', '''Timovan''', '''Tolnate'''), also known as '''azaphenothiazine''' or '''phrenotropin''', is an [[anxiolytic]], [[antiemetic]], and [[antihistamine]] of the [[phenothiazine|azaphenothiazine]] group which is marketed in [[Europe]] and is used to treat [[anxiety]] and [[psychomotor agitation|agitation]] in [[psychosis|psychotic syndrome]]s.<ref name="Elks2014">{{cite book|author=J. Elks|title=The Dictionary of Drugs: Chemical Data: Chemical Data, Structures and Bibliographies|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0vXTBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA1038|date=14 November 2014|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4757-2085-3|pages=1038–}}</ref><ref name="IndexNominum2000">{{cite book|title=Index Nominum 2000: International Drug Directory|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5GpcTQD_L2oC&pg=PA893|year=2000|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-3-88763-075-1|pages=893–}}</ref><ref name="Drugs.com">https://www.drugs.com/international/prothipendyl.html</ref><ref name="LeighPare2012">{{cite book|author1=D. Leigh|author2=C.M. Pare|author3=J. Marks|title=A Concise Encyclopaedia of Psychiatry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=o-bzCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA396|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-94-011-5913-5|pages=396–}}</ref> It differs from [[promazine]] only by the replacement of one [[carbon]] atom with a [[nitrogen]] atom in the [[tricyclic compound|tricyclic]] [[ring system (chemistry)|ring system]].<ref name="Elks2014" /><ref name="IndexNominum2000" /> Prothipendyl is said to not possess [[antipsychotic]] effects, and in accordance, appears to be a weaker [[dopamine receptor]] [[receptor antagonist|antagonist]] than other phenothiazines.<ref name="Springer2012">{{cite book|title=Psychotropic Agents: Part I: Antipsychotics and Antidepressants|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oK7tCAAAQBAJ&pg=PA131|date=6 December 2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|isbn=978-3-642-67538-6|pages=131–}}</ref><ref name="LeighPare2012" />
