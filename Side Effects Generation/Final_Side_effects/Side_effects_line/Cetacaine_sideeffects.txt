Cetacaine is an anesthetic that is a Benzocaine based drug that also contains other active ingredients that include Butamben and Tetracaine Hydrochloride.<ref name=":3">{{cite web |title=Cetacaine Topical Anesthetic - FDA prescribing information, side effects and uses |url=http://www.drugs.com/pro/cetacaine-topical-anesthetic.html |website=www.drugs.com |accessdate=2015-12-13}}</ref> The main use for this drug is to produce anesthesia to [[Mucous membrane|mucus membranes]] to numb and help control the pain in that area.<ref name=":2" /> The spray form of Cetacaine is also used to help prevent gagging in the patient.<ref name=":0" /> The anesthetic effect of Cetacaine can be expected to take effect in about 30 seconds and last between 30â€“60 minutes depending on location and application amount.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> Cetacaine can and has been used for surgeries that include bronchi, ear, esophagus, larynx, mouth, nose, pharynx, rectal, and vaginal procedures.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3" /> These procedures can include periodontal treatment, pre-probing, pre-scaling/root planning procedures, pre-injection, and laser dentistry.<ref name=":2" />

 
=== Adverse effects ===

 
Cetacaine has been known to cause adverse effects in the patients it has been administered to. These include [[hypersensitivity]] in the form of anaphylaxis, dermatitis, erythema, pruritus which can lead to oozing and vesiculation.<ref name=":3" />  There have also been accounts of rashes, edema, urticarial and other allergic symptoms as well as methemoglobinemia.<ref name=":1" /> Other adverse effects can include: tremors, twitching, dizziness, confusion, hypo-tension, vomiting, euphoria, and blurred or double vision.<ref name=":4">{{cite web |title=Cetacaine (benzocaine/butamben/tetracaine topical) dosing, indications, interactions, adverse effects, and more. |url=http://reference.medscape.com/drug/cetacaine-benzocaine-butamben-tetracaine-topical-999521#4 |website=reference.medscape.com |accessdate=2015-12-13}}</ref>

 
This product should not be used to cover a large area for anesthetic affect causing an adverse reaction.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cetacaine (benzocaine/butamben/tetracaine topical) dosing, indications, interactions, adverse effects, and more. |url=http://reference.medscape.com/drug/cetacaine-benzocaine-butamben-tetracaine-topical-999521#5 |website=reference.medscape.com |accessdate=2015-12-13}}</ref> The liquid and other forms of Cetacaine should not be administered via injection or used under dentures, on eyes or with patients with a cholinesterase deficiency.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":3" />
