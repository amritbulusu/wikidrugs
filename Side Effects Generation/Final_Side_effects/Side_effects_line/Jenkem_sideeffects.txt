The effects of jenkem inhalation last for around an hour and consist of auditory and visual [[hallucinations]] for some users.<ref name=BBC/> In 1995, one user told a reporter it is "more potent than [[cannabis]]."<ref name=IPS>{{cite news |title=Zambia-Narcotics: 'Huffing and Puffing' to a new High |publisher=[[Inter Press Service]] |date=August 26, 1995}} (reprinted in a Snopes.com [http://www.snopes.com/crime/warnings/jenkem.asp article on jenkem], retrieved December 30, 2007.)</ref> A 1999 report interviewed a user, who said, "With glue, I just hear voices in my head. But with jenkem, I see visions. I see my mother who is dead and I forget about the problems in my life."<ref name=BBC/> Fumito Ichinose, an anesthesia specialist in Boston who conducted a study on the effects of [[hydrogen sulfide]] gas, or "sewer gas," on mice, informed [[Salon (website)|Salon.com]] that holding your breath, choking, or "the inhalation of gases like those produced from jenkem could result in [[hypoxia (medical)|hypoxia]], a lack of oxygen flow to the body that could be alternately euphoric and physically dangerous."<ref name=Salon/>
