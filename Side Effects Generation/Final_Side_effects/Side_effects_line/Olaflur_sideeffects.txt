'''Olaflur''' ([[International Nonproprietary Name|INN]], or '''amine fluoride 297''') is a [[fluoride]]-containing substance that is an ingredient of toothpastes and solutions for the prevention of [[dental caries]].<ref>{{Cite journal | last1 = Ganss | first1 = C. | last2 = Klimek | first2 = J. | last3 = Brune | first3 = V. | last4 = Schürmann | first4 = A. | title = Effects of Two Fluoridation Measures on Erosion Progression in Human Enamel and Dentine in situ | doi = 10.1159/000080587 | journal = Caries Research | volume = 38 | issue = 6 | pages = 561–566 | year = 2004 | pmid =  15528912| pmc = }}</ref> It has been in use since 1966. Especially in combination with [[dectaflur]], it is also used in the form of gels for the treatment of early stages of caries, sensitive [[human tooth|teeth]], and by [[dentist]]s for the refluoridation of damaged [[tooth enamel]].<ref name="AustriaCodex">{{cite book|title=Austria-Codex|editor=Haberfeld, H|publisher=Österreichischer Apothekerverlag|location=Vienna|year=2009|edition=2009/2010|isbn=3-85200-196-X|language=German}}</ref>
